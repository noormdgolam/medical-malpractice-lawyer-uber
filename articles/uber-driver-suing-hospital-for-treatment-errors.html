Uber Driver Suing Hospital for Treatment Errors: A Guide to Medical Malpractice Claims

Understanding Medical Malpractice Claims in Rideshare Accidents

As an Uber driver, you trust that the medical professionals treating you will provide the highest standard of care. However, if you've been injured in a rideshare accident and received subpar treatment at a hospital, you may be entitled to compensation. This guide will walk you through the process of filing a medical malpractice claim against both the hospital and the Uber company.

Actionable Steps to Take in the First 24 Hours

  • Contact your insurance company and report the incident
  • Seek immediate medical attention at a different hospital
  • Document all medical records and bills related to the incident
  • Take photos and videos of any injuries or damage
  • Save all receipts and records of expenses related to the incident

Evidentiary Requirements for Medical Malpractice Claims

To succeed in a medical malpractice claim, you'll need to provide evidence that the hospital or medical professional deviated from the standard of care. This may include:

  • Medical records and testimony from experts
  • Expert witness testimony from medical professionals
  • Photographic and video evidence of injuries or damage
  • Testimony from witnesses, including Uber passengers or other drivers

Composite Case Study: Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

Ms. Johnson, a 35-year-old Uber driver, was involved in a rideshare accident and taken to a local hospital for treatment. Despite her severe injuries, the hospital failed to diagnose and treat her correctly, leading to further complications. Ms. Johnson's medical malpractice attorney filed a claim against both the hospital and Uber, alleging dual liability for the driver's injuries.

Through expert testimony and medical records, the attorney was able to demonstrate that the hospital had deviated from the standard of care, while also showing that Uber's failure to provide adequate support and resources to drivers contributed to the incident. The case settled for $1.2 million, with the hospital paying 60% and Uber paying 40%.

Source: Centers for Disease Control and Prevention estimates that medical errors result in 10% of all deaths in the United States.

What to Expect in the Medical Malpractice Claims Process

Filing a medical malpractice claim can be a complex and time-consuming process. Here's what you can expect:

  • Initial consultation with a medical malpractice attorney
  • Investigation and gathering of evidence
  • Pre-litigation settlement discussions with defendants
  • Litigation and trial, if necessary
  • Settlement or verdict, including compensation for medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering

Resources for Victims of Medical Malpractice

If you or a loved one has been a victim of medical malpractice, there are resources available to help:

  • Health Resources and Services Administration (HRSA) provides information on medical malpractice and patient safety
  • American Medical Association (AMA) offers resources on medical ethics and patient safety
