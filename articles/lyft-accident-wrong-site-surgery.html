Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I sue both the hospital and Lyft for the same injury?

Yes. In many jurisdictions, a plaintiff may bring separate claims against each negligent party. The hospital may be liable for the surgical error, while Lyft could be held responsible for the initial accident that precipitated the need for emergency surgery. However, the claims must be distinct, and the damages recovered from each defendant may be reduced to avoid â??double recovery.â?ť An experienced attorney can navigate these complexities and ensure you receive the maximum compensation allowed by law.

What is the statute of limitations for filing a medical malpractice claim?

The statute of limitations varies by state, typically ranging from one to three years from the date of injury or discovery of the injury. Because the clock can start ticking as soon as you become aware of the malpractice, it is prudent to consult an attorney promptly after the incident. Missing the filing deadline can bar you from ever receiving compensation.

Most personal injury and medical malpractice attorneys work on a contingency fee basis, meaning you do not pay any upfront legal fees.
