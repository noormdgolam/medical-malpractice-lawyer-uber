Uber Passenger Facial Scarring From Botched Surgery

By Editorial Team | Last Updated: July 17, 2026

Uber Passenger Facial Scarring from Botched Surgery: A Comprehensive Guide to Pursuing Justice

Introduction

As an Uber passenger, you trust the ride-sharing service to get you from point A to point B safely. However, what happens when a medical emergency arises during the ride? In this guide, we will explore the complexities of pursuing a medical malpractice claim when an Uber passenger suffers facial scarring from a botched surgery.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Contact the hospital and Uber immediately to report the incident and seek medical attention.
  • Document all communication with hospital staff, Uber representatives, and any other relevant parties.
  • Seek immediate medical attention to assess and treat any injuries.
  • Preserve all medical records, including test results, diagnoses, and treatment plans.
  • Consult with a medical malpractice attorney to discuss your options and potential claims.

Evidentiary Requirements

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you will need to demonstrate the following:

  • Duty: Show that the hospital and/or medical staff owed you a duty of care.
  • Breach: Prove that the hospital and/or medical staff breached their duty of care through negligence or incompetence.
  • Causation: Establish that the breach of duty caused your facial scarring.
  • Damages: Document the extent of your injuries and resulting damages, including medical expenses, pain and suffering, and emotional distress.

Composite Case Study: Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2020, a 35-year-old woman suffered severe facial scarring after undergoing a botched surgery at a hospital while being transported by Uber. The hospital's anesthesiologist failed to properly monitor the patient's vital signs, resulting in a prolonged anesthesia duration. The Uber driver, who was not trained in emergency medical procedures, failed to provide adequate care during the incident.

The woman's medical malpractice attorney successfully argued that both the hospital and Uber were liable for her injuries. The hospital was held accountable for the anesthesiologist's negligence, while Uber was found responsible for failing to provide adequate emergency medical training to its drivers. The case settled for $1.5 million, with $750,000 allocated to the hospital and $750,000 to Uber.
