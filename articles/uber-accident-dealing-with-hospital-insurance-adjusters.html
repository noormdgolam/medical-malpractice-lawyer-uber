Uber Accident: Dealing with Hospital Insurance Adjusters

Understanding Your Rights and Obligations

As a victim of an Uber accident, navigating the complex process of dealing with hospital insurance adjusters can be overwhelming. It's essential to understand your rights and obligations to ensure you receive fair compensation for your injuries.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek Medical Attention Immediately: Your health and safety are the top priority. Visit the emergency room or a nearby hospital to receive immediate medical attention.
  • Document Everything: Keep a record of all medical treatments, hospital stays, and communication with insurance adjusters.
  • Contact Your Insurance Company: Inform your insurance provider about the accident and provide them with the necessary documentation.
  • Consult with a Medical Malpractice Attorney: A qualified attorney can guide you through the process and ensure you receive fair compensation for your injuries.
  • Review and Understand Your Insurance Policy: Familiarize yourself with your insurance policy, including coverage limits, deductibles, and any exclusions.

Evidentiary Requirements

To establish liability and receive compensation, you'll need to gather evidence, including:

  • Police Report: Obtain a copy of the police report, which will detail the accident and any injuries sustained.
  • Medical Records: Collect all medical records, including diagnoses, treatments, and hospital stays.
  • Witness Statements: Gather statements from witnesses, including Uber drivers, passengers, and bystanders.
  • Photographic Evidence: Collect photographs of the accident scene, injuries, and any property damage.
  • Uber Accident Report: Obtain a copy of the Uber accident report, which will detail the circumstances surrounding the accident.

Case Study: Dual-Liability Settlement

Meet Jane Doe, a 35-year-old marketing executive who was involved in a serious Uber accident. She suffered severe head and neck injuries, requiring multiple surgeries and extensive rehabilitation. The Uber driver was found to be at fault, and the hospital where Jane was treated was accused of medical negligence. Through the efforts of her medical malpractice attorney, Jane was able to secure a dual-liability settlement of $2.5 million, with $1.5 million from the Uber insurance company and $1 million from the hospital's insurance provider.
