Can I Sue Both Uber and the Hospital for Negligence?

As a victim of medical malpractice, navigating the complex world of liability can be daunting. If you were involved in an accident while using a rideshare service like Uber, and subsequently received inadequate medical care at a hospital, you may be wondering if you can sue both parties for negligence. In this article, we will explore the possibility of dual-liability and provide actionable step-by-step advice on what to do in the first 24 hours.

Understanding Negligence and Liability

Negligence occurs when a person or organization fails to exercise reasonable care, resulting in harm or injury to another. In the context of a rideshare accident, Uber may be liable for negligence if their driver was responsible for the accident. Similarly, a hospital may be liable for negligence if their medical staff failed to provide adequate care, resulting in further harm or injury.

Evidentiary Requirements

To prove negligence, you will need to gather evidence to support your claim. This may include:

  • Medical records and bills
  • Police reports and accident reports
  • Witness statements and testimony
  • Photographic and video evidence of the accident and injuries
  • Expert testimony from medical professionals and accident reconstruction specialists

It's essential to document everything, including dates, times, and details of the accident and subsequent medical treatment. You can also consult with a medical malpractice attorney to ensure you have a comprehensive understanding of the evidentiary requirements.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

After an accident, it's crucial to take immediate action to protect your rights and gather evidence. Follow these steps within the first 24 hours:

  • Seek medical attention immediately, even if you feel fine.
  • Document the accident, including photos, videos, and witness statements.
  • Report the accident to Uber and the hospital.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney to discuss your case and gather evidence.
  • Preserve all medical records and bills.

Composite Case Study: Dual-Liability Settlement

A 35-year-old woman, Sarah, was involved in a car accident while using Uber. She suffered severe injuries and was taken to a nearby hospital for treatment. However, the hospital's medical staff failed to diagnose her condition correctly, leading to further complications. Sarah's medical bills exceeded $200,000, and she suffered significant emotional distress.

Sarah hired a medical malpractice attorney who filed a lawsuit against both Uber and the hospital. The case was settled out of court for $750,000, with Uber paying $300,000 and the hospital paying $450,000.

According to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA), ride-sharing accidents are on the rise. In 2020, there were over 3,000 reported ride-sharing accidents in the United States, resulting in over 1,000 injuries and 100 fatalities.

The American Medical Association (AMA) emphasizes the importance of accurate and complete medical records in medical malpractice cases. A thorough medical record can help establish a patient's medical history, treatment, and outcomes, which are crucial in determining liability.
