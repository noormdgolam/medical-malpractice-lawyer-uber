Uber Crash Surgical Instrument Left In Body: A Guide to Medical Malpractice and Rideshare Liability

Understanding the Dangers of Medical Malpractice and Rideshare Liability

When an Uber crash occurs, the consequences can be devastating. In addition to the physical harm caused by the accident, victims may also face medical malpractice if a surgical instrument is left inside their body during treatment. This guide will walk you through the steps to take if you or a loved one has been a victim of an Uber crash and medical malpractice.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek Medical Attention Immediately: If you've been injured in an Uber crash, seek medical attention right away. Even if you don't feel like you've been seriously hurt, it's essential to get checked out by a medical professional.
  • Document Everything: Keep a record of all medical treatment, including doctor's visits, hospital stays, and any medications prescribed. This documentation will be crucial in building your case.
  • Report the Incident to Uber and the Police: File a report with Uber and the police department to ensure that the incident is documented and investigated.
  • Consult with a Medical Malpractice Attorney: Reach out to a qualified medical malpractice attorney who can guide you through the process and help you navigate the complex laws surrounding medical malpractice and rideshare liability.

Evidentiary Requirements

To establish medical malpractice, you'll need to prove that a healthcare provider deviated from the standard of care, resulting in harm or injury. In the case of a surgical instrument being left inside the body, you'll need to provide evidence that:

  • The healthcare provider failed to follow proper surgical protocols
  • The failure to follow protocols resulted in the instrument being left inside the body
  • The instrument caused harm or injury to the patient

Realistic Composite Case Study

A 35-year-old woman was involved in an Uber crash and suffered a severe head injury. She was rushed to the hospital, where she underwent emergency surgery to relieve pressure on her brain. During the surgery, a surgical instrument was left inside her body. Several days later, she began to experience severe abdominal pain and was diagnosed with a bowel obstruction caused by the retained instrument. She underwent additional surgery to remove the instrument and repair the damage. She suffered significant physical and emotional trauma, including a permanent scar and ongoing abdominal pain.

The woman hired a medical malpractice attorney who filed a lawsuit against the hospital and the surgeon, alleging that they deviated from the standard of care by leaving the instrument inside her body. The case settled for $1.5 million, with Uber also being held liable for their role in the accident.

According to the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services (CMS), medical malpractice is a leading cause of preventable harm in healthcare. The American Medical Association (AMA) also notes that medical malpractice can result in significant physical and emotional harm to patients.

Conclusion

If you or a loved one has been a victim of an Uber crash and medical malpractice, it's essential to seek the guidance of a qualified medical malpractice attorney. By following the steps outlined in this guide and providing evidence of medical malpractice, you may be able to secure the compensation you deserve and hold those responsible accountable for their actions.
