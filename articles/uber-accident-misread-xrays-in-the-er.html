What to Do in the First 24 Hours

  • Contact your Uber driver and the hospital immediately to report the incident and any subsequent medical treatment.
  • Seek a second opinion from a specialist if you're not satisfied with your initial diagnosis or treatment.
  • Document all medical records, including X-rays, lab results, and any correspondence with your healthcare providers.
  • Consult with an experienced medical malpractice attorney to discuss your options and potential claims.
  • Report the incident to the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) and the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) if you believe the accident was caused by a vehicle defect or driver error.

Evidentiary Requirements for Medical Malpractice Claims

To succeed in a medical malpractice claim, you'll need to establish the following elements:

  • Duty: The healthcare provider had a duty to provide a standard level of care.
  • Breach: The healthcare provider breached that duty by misreading your X-rays or providing subpar care.
  • Causation: The breach caused your injuries or exacerbated your condition.
  • Damages: You suffered damages as a result of the breach, such as medical expenses, lost wages, or pain and suffering.

Each element must be supported by concrete evidence. For duty and breach, expert testimony from a boardâ??certified radiologist or orthopedic surgeon is typically required.
