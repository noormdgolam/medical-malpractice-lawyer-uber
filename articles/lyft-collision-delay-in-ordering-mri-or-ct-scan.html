Understanding Your Rights: A Comprehensive Guide to Lyft Collision Delay in Ordering MRI or CT Scan

Introduction

As a rideshare passenger, you expect a safe and timely ride to your destination. However, when a collision occurs, the consequences can be severe, and timely medical attention is crucial. If you've experienced a delay in ordering an MRI or CT scan after a Lyft collision, you may be entitled to compensation. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to take and the evidence you need to gather to pursue a medical malpractice claim.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek medical attention immediately: Even if you feel fine, it's essential to get checked out by a medical professional as soon as possible.
  • Document everything: Take photos of the accident scene, your injuries, and any damage to the vehicle. Also, keep a record of all medical treatment, including dates, times, and descriptions of procedures.
  • Notify Lyft and your insurance company: Report the incident to both Lyft and your insurance provider, even if you're not sure if you'll file a claim.
  • Preserve evidence: Keep all medical records, police reports, and any other relevant documents related to the accident and your treatment.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney: Reach out to an experienced attorney who can guide you through the process and help you navigate any potential claims.

Evidentiary Requirements

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you'll need to demonstrate that the healthcare provider's delay in ordering an MRI or CT scan resulted in harm or injury. This may involve:

  • Expert testimony: A medical expert will need to testify that the delay in ordering an MRI or CT scan was a deviation from the standard of care.
  • Medical records: You'll need to provide detailed medical records, including test results, diagnoses, and treatment plans.
  • Witness statements: Statements from witnesses, including first responders and medical personnel, may be necessary to corroborate your account of the events.
  • Photographic evidence: Photos of the accident scene, injuries, and any damage to the vehicle can help establish the severity of the incident.
