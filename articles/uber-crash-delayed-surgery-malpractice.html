Uber's Liability in Crash Delays

California's AB5 law, enacted in 2020, codified the distinction between employees and independent contractors in the gig economy. This has significant implications for cases involving Uber's liability in delayed medical treatment following accidents, as we'll explore below. ### Uber's Response to Lawsuits and Liability A recent case highlights the complexities of Uber's liability in delayed medical treatment. In the 2020 Uber crash mentioned above, a passenger's gallbladder surgery was delayed due to the accident. The passenger subsequently brought a lawsuit against Uber, alleging that the company's negligence in delaying treatment led to additional medical costs and suffering. In response, Uber claimed that the passenger's injuries were not caused by the accident itself but by the subsequent delay in medical treatment. The case raises questions about the boundaries of Uber's liability and whether the company can be held accountable for delays in medical treatment following an accident.

Medical Malpractice vs. Negligence of Care Delay

Medical malpractice and negligence of care delay are often considered separate concepts, but they can intersect or diverge depending on the circumstances of the case. ### Medical Malpractice Medical malpractice refers to substandard medical care that causes harm to a patient. This can include errors in diagnosis, treatment, or aftercare, as well as failure to obtain informed consent. ### Negligence of Care Delay Negligence of care delay, on the other hand, refers to a failure to provide timely medical treatment or care that results in harm to the patient. This can include delays in diagnosis, treatment, or aftercare, as well as failure to meet hospital or medical facility standards.
