Uber Accident Nurse Negligence in ER: A Comprehensive Guide for Victims

Understanding the Complexity of Medical Malpractice Claims

As an Uber accident victim, navigating the complexities of medical malpractice claims can be overwhelming. When a nurse in the emergency room (ER) fails to provide adequate care, it can lead to severe consequences, including permanent injuries, disabilities, or even death.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek immediate medical attention at a nearby hospital, even if you feel fine. This ensures your injuries are properly assessed and treated.
  • Document everything: take photos of your injuries, gather contact information from witnesses, and save any relevant medical records.
  • Report the incident to Uber and provide a detailed account of what happened.
  • Consult with a medical malpractice attorney who specializes in ER negligence cases.
  • Preserve evidence: do not delete any text messages, emails, or social media posts related to the incident.

Evidentiary Requirements for Medical Malpractice Claims

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you must prove the following:

  • Duty of care**: The nurse or hospital owed you a duty of care, which was breached due to negligence.
  • Breach of duty**: The nurse or hospital failed to meet the standard of care, resulting in your injuries.
  • Causation**: The breach of duty directly caused your injuries or worsened your condition.
  • Damages**: You suffered damages, including medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

Composite Case Study: Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2019, a 35-year-old woman was involved in a rideshare accident while on her way to the hospital for a scheduled C-section. During the ER admission process, the nurse failed to properly assess her injuries, leading to a delayed diagnosis of a spinal cord injury.

The woman suffered permanent paralysis and was awarded a $2.5 million settlement in a dual-liability case against both the rideshare company and the hospital. The settlement covered her medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.
