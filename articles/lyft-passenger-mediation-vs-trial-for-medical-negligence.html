Lyft Passenger Mediation Vs Trial For Medical Negligence: A Guide to Navigating Dual-Liability Claims

Understanding the Risks of Medical Negligence in Rideshare Accidents

Rideshare accidents can result in severe injuries, and in some cases, medical negligence can exacerbate the situation. If you're a Lyft passenger who has suffered an injury due to medical negligence, you may be entitled to compensation from both Lyft and the hospital or medical facility that provided treatment. In this article, we'll explore the options available to you, including mediation and trial, and provide guidance on how to navigate the complex process of dual-liability claims.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice for the First 24 Hours

  • Seek medical attention immediately after the accident.
  • Document all injuries and treatment received.
  • Notify Lyft and the hospital or medical facility of the incident.
  • Preserve all evidence, including photos, videos, and witness statements.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney with experience in dual-liability claims.

Evidentiary Requirements for Medical Negligence Claims

To establish a medical negligence claim, you'll need to provide evidence of the following:

  • A breach of the standard of care by the healthcare provider.
  • Causation, meaning that the breach of care directly caused your injuries.
  • Damages, which can include medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

Mediation vs Trial: Which Option is Right for You?

Mediation is a process where a neutral third-party facilitates a discussion between you and Lyft and/or the hospital or medical facility to reach a settlement. This can be a faster and less expensive option than going to trial. However, if mediation is not successful, you may need to pursue a trial to obtain the compensation you deserve.

Composite Case Study: A Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

Sarah, a 35-year-old Lyft passenger, was involved in a car accident while riding in a Lyft vehicle. She suffered a severe head injury and was taken to a local hospital for treatment. Unfortunately, the hospital's emergency room staff failed to properly diagnose and treat Sarah's injury, resulting in further complications and additional medical expenses. Sarah's medical malpractice attorney successfully negotiated a settlement with both Lyft and the hospital, resulting in a total award of $750,000.

Lyft was held liable for 40% of the award, while the hospital was held liable for 60%. The settlement included compensation for Sarah's medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering, as well as punitive damages against the hospital for their negligence.
