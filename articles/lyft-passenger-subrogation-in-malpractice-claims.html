Lyft Passenger Subrogation In Malpractice Claims: A Comprehensive Guide

Understanding the Basics of Subrogation

As a Lyft passenger who has been injured due to medical malpractice, you may be entitled to compensation for your damages. However, navigating the complex process of subrogation can be daunting. In this article, we will provide you with a comprehensive guide on how to proceed with your claim.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek immediate medical attention for your injuries. Document all medical records, including test results and treatment plans.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney who has experience handling subrogation claims. They will guide you through the process and ensure that your rights are protected.
  • Notify Lyft of the incident and provide them with any relevant documentation. They may have a claims process in place for passengers.
  • Document all communication with Lyft, including emails, phone calls, and meetings.
  • Do not sign any settlement agreements or release forms without consulting with your attorney.

Evidentiary Requirements

To establish a subrogation claim, you will need to provide evidence of the following:

  • A clear diagnosis of your injuries and the extent of your damages.
  • Documentation of your medical treatment, including hospital records, doctor's notes, and test results.
  • Proof of the cause of your injuries, including witness statements, police reports, and any other relevant evidence.
  • Documentation of your financial losses, including medical bills, lost wages, and other expenses.

Composite Case Study: Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2019, a Lyft passenger was involved in a car accident caused by a drunk driver. The passenger was rushed to the hospital, where they received inadequate care, leading to further complications and additional injuries. The passenger filed a lawsuit against both the drunk driver and the hospital, alleging medical malpractice and seeking damages for their injuries. The passenger's attorney worked with Lyft to establish the passenger's subrogation claim. The attorney gathered evidence, including medical records, witness statements, and financial documentation, to support the claim. After negotiations, the passenger received a settlement of $1.2 million from the hospital and $500,000 from Lyft's insurance company. The passenger's attorney was able to recover 80% of the total settlement amount, leaving the passenger with a significant portion of the compensation they deserved.
