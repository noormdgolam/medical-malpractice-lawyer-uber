Uber Accident Misread X-Rays In The ER: A Comprehensive Guide for Victims

Understanding the Risks and Consequences

When you're involved in an Uber accident, the last thing you expect is to receive subpar medical care at the emergency room. Unfortunately, medical misreadings and misdiagnoses can happen even in the most critical situations. In this article, we'll explore the risks and consequences of misread X-rays in the ER and provide actionable step-by-step advice for victims.

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

  • Contact your Uber driver and the hospital immediately to report the incident and any subsequent medical treatment.
  • Seek a second opinion from a specialist if you're not satisfied with your initial diagnosis or treatment.
  • Document all medical records, including X-rays, lab results, and any correspondence with your healthcare providers.
  • Consult with an experienced medical malpractice attorney to discuss your options and potential claims.
  • Report the incident to the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB) and the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) if you believe the accident was caused by a vehicle defect or driver error.

Evidentiary Requirements for Medical Malpractice Claims

To succeed in a medical malpractice claim, you'll need to establish the following elements:

  • Duty: The healthcare provider had a duty to provide a standard level of care.
  • Breach: The healthcare provider breached that duty by misreading your X-rays or providing subpar care.
  • Causation: The breach caused your injuries or exacerbated your condition.
  • Damages: You suffered damages as a result of the breach, such as medical expenses, lost wages, or pain and suffering.

Composite Case Study: A Rideshare-Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2019, a 35-year-old woman was involved in an Uber accident that resulted in a serious back injury. She was taken to the emergency room, where her X-rays were misread, leading to a delayed diagnosis and inadequate treatment. As a result, her condition worsened, and she required multiple surgeries and extended physical therapy.

After consulting with an experienced medical malpractice attorney, she filed a lawsuit against both Uber and the hospital, alleging dual liability for the misread X-rays and subsequent subpar care. The case settled for $1.2 million, with $600,000 allocated to the hospital for medical malpractice and $600,000 allocated to Uber for their role in the accident.
