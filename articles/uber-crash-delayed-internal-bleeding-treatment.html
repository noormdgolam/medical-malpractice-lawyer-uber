Uber Crash Delayed Internal Bleeding Treatment: A Guide for Victims

Understanding the Risks of Medical Negligence

When an Uber crash delays internal bleeding treatment, the consequences can be severe and even life-threatening. As a medical malpractice attorney, I've seen firsthand the devastating impact of delayed medical care on patients and their families. In this guide, we'll walk you through the essential steps to take in the first 24 hours, the evidentiary requirements for a successful claim, and a composite case study of a similar rideshare/hospital dual-liability settlement.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek immediate medical attention if you or a loved one has been involved in an Uber crash and is experiencing symptoms of internal bleeding, such as severe abdominal pain, vomiting, or difficulty breathing.
  • Document everything: EMT checklist and medical records, including any treatment provided at the scene and in the hospital.
  • Preserve evidence: Take photos of any injuries, vehicle damage, and the accident scene. Also, save any relevant text messages, emails, or social media posts related to the incident.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney: Reach out to an experienced attorney who can guide you through the complex process of filing a claim and help you navigate the system.

Evidentiary Requirements

To establish a successful medical malpractice claim, you'll need to provide evidence of the following:

  • Duty of care: Proof that the hospital or medical provider had a duty to provide timely and adequate medical care.
  • Breach of duty: Evidence that the hospital or medical provider breached their duty of care, resulting in delayed treatment of internal bleeding.
  • Causation: Proof that the delayed treatment caused harm or injury to the patient.
  • Damages: Documentation of the patient's injuries, medical expenses, and any other related damages.

Composite Case Study: Dual-Liability Settlement

Meet Jane Doe, a 35-year-old mother of two who was involved in an Uber crash on her way home from work. The Uber driver lost control of the vehicle, and Jane suffered severe internal bleeding due to a ruptured spleen. Despite her symptoms, the hospital delayed treatment for over an hour, resulting in further complications and a longer recovery time.

Jane's medical malpractice attorney worked tirelessly to gather evidence and build a strong case against both the hospital and the Uber company. The attorney obtained records of the hospital's internal policies and procedures, as well as testimony from medical experts who opined that the delayed treatment was a deviation from the standard of care.

After a lengthy settlement negotiation, Jane's attorney was able to secure a dual-liability settlement of $2.5 million from both the hospital and Uber. The settlement covered Jane's medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

"I'm just grateful that my attorney was able to hold both the hospital and Uber accountable for their negligence," Jane said. "It's a relief to know that my family and I will be able to move forward without the burden of medical debt."

Conclusion

When an Uber crash delays internal bleeding treatment, the consequences can be severe. By understanding the risks of medical negligence and taking immediate action, victims can increase their chances of a successful claim. Remember to document everything, preserve evidence, and contact a medical malpractice attorney who can guide you through the complex process of filing a claim.
