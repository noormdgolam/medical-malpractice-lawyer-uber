Lyft Accident Doctor Failing To Diagnose Concussion: A Guide for Victims

Understanding Your Rights and Options

As a rideshare passenger, you trust that the driver and medical professionals will prioritize your safety and well‚??being. However, when a doctor fails to diagnose a concussion after a Lyft accident, you may be entitled to compensation for your injuries and suffering. The law recognises that a missed diagnosis can be as damaging as a physical injury caused by the collision itself, because delayed treatment often leads to prolonged symptoms, lost wages, and a diminished quality of life.

Under most state statutes, patients have the right to pursue a medical malpractice claim when a healthcare provider breaches the standard of care. In the context of rideshare accidents, this right extends to any physician who evaluates you in the emergency department, urgent care clinic, or even a private practice after the incident. If the physician neglects to order appropriate imaging (such as a CT scan) or fails to perform a thorough neurological exam, that omission can be considered negligent.

  • Document all medical records, including test results, diagnoses, and treatment plans.

These steps are critical because early documentation creates a clear timeline that can be used to demonstrate how the missed diagnosis impacted your recovery. In many jurisdictions, the ‚??statute of limitations‚?ù for medical malpractice begins when the injury is discovered, not when the accident occurred, so acting quickly safeguards your legal options.

Evidentiary Requirements for a Medical Malpractice Claim

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you must demonstrate that the doctor's failure to diagnose the concussion was a deviation from the standard of care, resulting in harm or injury.

  • Expert testimony from a medical professional who can attest to the standard of care and the doctor's deviation from it.
  • Medical records and test results that confirm the doctor's failure to diagnose the concussion.
  • Documentation of your injuries and treatment, including any subsequent complications or worsening of symptoms.
  • Witness statements or testimony from other medical professionals who can corroborate your account.

In addition to the above, a plaintiff often needs to show causation‚??that the missed diagnosis directly caused the exacerbated symptoms. For example, if a CT scan was omitted and the patient later suffers a hematoma that could have been detected early, that link becomes a powerful piece of evidence.

How to Prove Medical Negligence in Court

Proving negligence revolves around four key elements: duty, breach, causation, and damages. Below is a practical roadmap for each element as it applies to a missed concussion diagnosis:

  1. Duty: The physician owed you a duty to provide care that meets the accepted medical standard for evaluating head trauma after a motor vehicle accident.
  2. Breach: Evidence such as the absence of a neurological exam, failure to order a CT scan, or ignoring documented symptoms (e.g., loss of consciousness) shows a breach.
  3. Causation: Expert testimony links the missed diagnosis to the worsening of symptoms‚??e.g., a neurologist explains how early detection could have prevented chronic PCS.
  4. Damages: Medical bills, lost wages, future earning potential, and non‚??economic damages like pain and suffering are quantified through detailed records and economic analysis.

Many courts also consider ‚??res ipsa loquitur‚?ù (the thing speaks for itself) when the injury would not ordinarily occur without negligence. A missed concussion often meets this threshold because a competent physician would normally detect it during an initial assessment.
