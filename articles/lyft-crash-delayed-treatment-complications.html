Lyft Crash Delayed Treatment Complications: A Medical Malpractice Guide

Understanding the Risks of Delayed Medical Treatment After a Lyft Crash

As a rideshare passenger, you trust the driver to get you to your destination safely. However, when a Lyft crash occurs, the consequences can be severe. In some cases, delayed medical treatment can exacerbate injuries, leading to long-term complications. If you or a loved one has experienced delayed treatment after a Lyft crash, you may be entitled to compensation.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice: What to Do in the First 24 Hours

  • Seek immediate medical attention if you are injured in a Lyft crash. Even if you feel fine, it's essential to get checked out by a medical professional to identify potential injuries.
  • Document everything related to the crash, including photos, witness statements, and police reports.
  • Notify Lyft of the incident and report any injuries or damages.
  • Seek legal representation from a medical malpractice attorney who specializes in rideshare accidents.
  • Keep detailed records of all medical treatments, including dates, times, and descriptions of procedures.

Evidentiary Requirements for a Successful Medical Malpractice Claim

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you'll need to demonstrate that the healthcare provider deviated from the standard of care, resulting in harm to the patient. The following elements are essential to a successful claim:

  • Standard of care: Establish the standard of care that a reasonable healthcare provider would have provided under similar circumstances.
  • Breach of duty: Show that the healthcare provider deviated from the standard of care, resulting in harm to the patient.
  • Causation: Prove that the breach of duty caused the patient's injuries or harm.
  • Damages: Document the extent of the patient's injuries or harm, including medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

Case Study: A Composite Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

Ms. Johnson was a 35-year-old mother of two who was injured in a Lyft crash. She was rushed to the hospital, where she received delayed treatment for her injuries. As a result, she developed a severe infection that required multiple surgeries and extended hospitalization. The hospital's failure to provide timely treatment led to significant complications, including permanent nerve damage and chronic pain.

Ms. Johnson's medical malpractice attorney worked with experts to establish that the hospital deviated from the standard of care, resulting in her injuries. The attorney also worked with Lyft to establish that the company was liable for the crash. After a lengthy settlement negotiation, Ms. Johnson received a $1.5 million settlement, which included compensation for her medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.
