Lyft Collision Pain And Suffering From Botched Surgery

Lyft Collision Pain And Suffering From Botched Surgery: A Comprehensive Guide to Seeking Justice

What You Need to Know

As a Lyft passenger, you trust that the ride-sharing service will get you safely from point A to point B. However, what happens when a collision occurs, and the resulting injuries require medical attention? If the medical treatment you receive is subpar, leading to further harm or complications, you may be entitled to compensation for your pain and suffering.

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

  • Contact Lyft's customer service to report the incident and obtain a claim number.
  • Seek immediate medical attention, even if you don't feel injured. Some injuries, such as whiplash, may not be apparent until hours or days later.
  • Document everything, including your injuries, medical treatment, and any communication with Lyft or medical providers.
  • Preserve any evidence, including photos of the accident scene, your injuries, and any medical equipment or devices used during treatment.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney experienced in handling cases involving rideshare accidents and botched surgeries.

Evidentiary Requirements

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you'll need to prove that the medical provider's negligence caused your injuries or further harm. This typically involves demonstrating:

  • A breach of the standard of care: Show that the medical provider failed to meet the accepted standard of care in your situation.
  • Causation: Prove that the medical provider's actions (or inactions) directly caused your injuries or further harm.
  • Damages: Establish the extent of your injuries, including physical, emotional, and financial losses.

Composite Case Study: A Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2020, a 35-year-old woman was involved in a Lyft collision that resulted in a severe head injury. She was taken to a local hospital, where she underwent emergency surgery. However, due to the hospital's negligence, the surgery was botched, leading to further complications and a prolonged recovery.

The woman's medical malpractice attorney filed a lawsuit against both Lyft and the hospital, alleging dual liability for her injuries. The case went to trial, and the jury awarded the woman $2.5 million in damages, with $1.5 million attributed to the hospital's negligence and $1 million attributed to Lyft's role in the collision.

This case highlights the importance of seeking justice not only from the medical provider but also from the rideshare company responsible for the initial accident.
