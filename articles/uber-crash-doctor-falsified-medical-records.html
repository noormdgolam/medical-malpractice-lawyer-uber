Uber Crash Doctor Falsified Medical Records: A Guide for Victims

Understanding Your Rights

As a victim of an Uber-related crash, you may be facing a complex web of medical and insurance issues. If you suspect that your doctor falsified your medical records, it's essential to take immediate action to protect your rights.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Contact your doctor and express your concerns about the medical records. Request a copy of your records and ask them to clarify any discrepancies.
  • Review your medical records carefully and take note of any inconsistencies or red flags. Consult with a medical professional if you're unsure about the accuracy of your records.
  • Report any concerns to the relevant authorities, such as the state medical board or the National Practitioner Data Bank (npdb.hrsa.gov).
  • Seek the advice of a medical malpractice attorney who specializes in cases involving falsified medical records.
  • Document everything: keep a record of all correspondence, phone calls, and meetings related to your case.

Evidentiary Requirements

To build a strong case against the doctor and Uber, you'll need to gather evidence that proves the falsification of medical records. This may include:

  • Expert testimony from a medical professional who can attest to the inaccuracies in your records.
  • Documentation of any discrepancies or red flags in your medical records.
  • Witness statements from family members, friends, or other healthcare professionals who may have observed the falsification of records.
  • Copies of your medical records, including any amendments or corrections made by the doctor.

Case Study: A Composite Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

Meet Jane Doe, a 35-year-old mother of two who was involved in a severe Uber crash in 2020. After being rushed to the hospital, Jane was treated by Dr. Smith, a prominent emergency medicine specialist. However, during her recovery, Jane began to notice inconsistencies in her medical records. She discovered that Dr. Smith had falsified her records to justify unnecessary treatments and procedures.

Jane hired a medical malpractice attorney who specialized in cases involving falsified medical records. Together, they built a strong case against Dr. Smith and Uber. The evidence included expert testimony from a medical professional, documentation of discrepancies in Jane's medical records, and witness statements from family members who observed the falsification of records.

After a lengthy investigation, the state medical board revoked Dr. Smith's medical license, and Uber was held liable for the crash. The court awarded Jane a substantial settlement, including compensation for medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering. The total settlement amount was $1.2 million, with Uber paying $600,000 and Dr. Smith's malpractice insurance paying the remaining $600,000.

As Jane said in a statement, "I'm just grateful that I have my life back, and I'm grateful for the justice that was served. I hope that my story will serve as a warning to others about the importance of verifying medical records and seeking help when something doesn't feel right."

Identifying Falsified Records: Red Flags to Watch For

Even if you are not a medical professional, certain patterns can signal that a record has been altered or fabricated. Recognizing these red flags early can save you months of costly litigation and protect your health.

Tips for Protecting Your Rights After an Uber Accident

Beyond addressing falsified records, there are broader steps you can take to safeguard your legal position:

  • Preserve all communications: Save text messages, emails, and voicemails exchanged with medical providers, Uber representatives, and insurance adjusters.
  • Seek a second medical opinion: An independent physician can verify diagnoses, treatment plans, and the authenticity of your records.
  • Maintain a symptom diary: Document pain levels, medication usage, and functional limitations daily. This journal can become critical evidence of ongoing impairment.
  • Limit public statements: Avoid discussing the case on social media or with anyone not directly involved in your representation, as statements can be used against you.
  • Engage a specialized attorney early: Lawyers experienced in rideshare and medical malpractice matters understand the nuances of jurisdictional statutes and can coordinate expert witnesses efficiently.

By following these guidelines, you not only strengthen your claim but also ensure that your health remains the top priority throughout the legal process.
