Uber Passenger Suing A Teaching Hospital For Negligence: A Comprehensive Guide

Introduction

As a passenger in an Uber, you trust that the driver will safely transport you to your destination. However, when an emergency situation arises, you may be taken to a nearby teaching hospital for medical attention. Unfortunately, medical errors can occur even in the best of hospitals, leading to severe injuries or even death. If you or a loved one has been a victim of medical negligence at a teaching hospital while riding in an Uber, you may be entitled to compensation.

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

  • Seek immediate medical attention for your injuries, even if they seem minor.
  • Document everything: take photos of your injuries, keep a record of your medical treatment, and save any relevant medical bills or receipts.
  • Contact your Uber driver and the hospital to report the incident and inquire about their policies and procedures.
  • Reach out to a medical malpractice attorney who specializes in cases involving teaching hospitals and ride-sharing services.
  • Preserve any evidence related to the incident, including security footage, witness statements, and medical records.

Evidentiary Requirements

In a medical malpractice case, you must establish that the hospital and/or medical staff deviated from the standard of care, resulting in harm to you or your loved one. To prove negligence, you will need to provide evidence of:

  • The applicable standard of care in the medical community.
  • The hospital and/or medical staff's failure to meet that standard.
  • The cause-and-effect relationship between the deviation from the standard and the harm suffered.
  • Damages resulting from the negligence, such as medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

It is essential to work with a qualified attorney who can help you gather and present this evidence in court.

Composite Case Study: A Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2018, a passenger in an Uber was taken to a teaching hospital in Los Angeles after experiencing severe abdominal pain. The hospital's emergency room staff failed to properly diagnose and treat the passenger's condition, leading to a delay in treatment and further complications. The passenger eventually underwent emergency surgery and suffered permanent damage to her intestines.

The passenger filed a lawsuit against the teaching hospital, alleging negligence and failure to meet the standard of care. The case was settled for $2.5 million, with the hospital's insurance carrier paying $1.5 million and the hospital itself contributing $1 million.

This case highlights the importance of holding teaching hospitals accountable for medical errors, even when they occur in emergency situations. By working with a qualified attorney, you can ensure that your rights are protected and that you receive the compensation you deserve.
