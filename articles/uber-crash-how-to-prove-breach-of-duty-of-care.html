Uber Crash How To Prove Breach Of Duty Of Care

By Editorial Team | Last Updated: July 17, 2026

Share:

Author: James Legal, Esq. | Last Updated: 07/17/2026

  • Umbrella policies offered by ride-sharing companies like Uber often shift liability from the company to the drivers in the event of a crash.
  • Drivers are considered independent contractors rather than employees, limiting the company's responsibility in cases of breach of duty of care.
  • Digital evidence from vehicle tracking data, the Uber app, and social media can be used to establish the sequence of events leading up to a crash.
  • Uber's inadequate hiring practices, inadequate training, and substandard vehicle safety requirements may contribute to a breach of duty of care in ride-sharing crashes.

Rider vs. Driver Liability: Uncovering the Gray Area

Understanding the Complexities of Ride-Sharing Liabilities

When a ride-sharing crash occurs, determining liability can be a daunting task. Ride-sharing companies like Uber operate under a complex model, blending elements of traditional employment with the characteristics of independent contractor relationships. This blurring of lines creates a gray area where it becomes challenging to distinguish between the company's and the driver's liabilities.

The 9th Circuit Court of Appeals Ruling in O'Connor v. Uber

In O'Connor v. Uber, the US 9th Circuit Court of Appeals ruled that Uber drivers are independent contractors rather than employees. While this decision may seem to eliminate the company's liability, it doesn't entirely absolve Uber from responsibility. The court's ruling highlights the importance of gathering evidence to demonstrate the company's breach of duty of care.
Key Considerations for Proving Breach of Duty of Care
1. Umbrella policies and insurance coverage: Companies like Uber often offer umbrella policies to protect their drivers in the event of a crash. However, these policies may shift liability from the company to the driver, making it essential to investigate the specific terms and conditions of the policy. 2. Investigating driver background: Inadequate background checks or hiring processes can contribute to a breach of duty of care. By examining the driver's history and hiring process, investigators can establish a potential connection to the crash. 3. Vehicle safety and maintenance: Ride-sharing companies have a responsibility to ensure their vehicles meet safety standards. If a crash occurs due to a vehicle-related issue, it may be possible to argue that the company breached its duty of care.

Negligent Hiring, Training, and Vehicle Safety

Ride-sharing companies like Uber have a critical role in maintaining a safe ride-sharing ecosystem. This includes ensuring that drivers undergo rigorous background checks, receive adequate training, and operate vehicles that meet strict safety standards.

Inadequate Hiring Practices

Uber has faced criticism for its inadequate hiring practices, including: 1. Inadequate background checks: Some reports have shown that Uber's background checks may not be comprehensive, potentially allowing drivers with violent or reckless histories to operate under the company's umbrella. 2. Lack of driver screening: Ride-sharing companies often rely on self-reported information from drivers, which can lead to inaccuracies and potential safety risks.
Training and Vehicle Safety Standards
Ride-sharing companies must prioritize driver training and vehicle safety. This includes: 1. First aid and emergency training: Drivers should be equipped with basic first aid skills to handle medical emergencies. 2. Vehicle maintenance and inspection: Ride-sharing companies must ensure their vehicles meet strict safety standards and undergo regular maintenance and inspections.

Digital Evidence and Vehicle Tracking

In the digital age, investigators can gather crucial evidence from various sources to establish the sequence of events leading up to a crash: 1. Vehicle tracking data: Many ride-sharing companies use GPS tracking systems to monitor driver activity. This data can provide valuable insights into the events surrounding a crash. 2. Digital evidence from the Uber app: Social media posts, ride details, and other app-related data can help investigators reconstruct the events leading up to a crash.

Recent Examples and Relevant Case Law

Several high-profile cases have shed light on the complex issues surrounding ride-sharing liability: 1. The 2015 Los Angeles ride-sharing crash: A fatal crash in Los Angeles highlighted the risks associated with ride-sharing and the need for enhanced safety measures. 2. The 2016 San Francisco self-driving car accident: A self-driving car crash in San Francisco raised questions about liability and the responsibilities of ride-sharing companies in cases involving autonomous vehicles. These examples demonstrate the importance of establishing a breach of duty of care in the event of a ride-sharing crash.

Conclusion

Determining liability in ride-sharing crashes can be a challenging task, with a complex interplay of company and driver responsibilities. By gathering evidence, understanding the nuances of ride-sharing liabilities, and leveraging digital tools, investigators can effectively demonstrate a breach of duty of care.

FAQ

Q: What is the primary challenge in determining liability in ride-sharing crashes?

A: The primary challenge lies in distinguishing between the company's and the driver's liabilities, as ride-sharing companies often operate under a complex model that blurs the lines between traditional employment and independent contractor relationships.

Q: Can I still hold Uber responsible for a breach of duty of care if the driver was independently contracted?

A: Yes, you may still be able to establish a breach of duty of care if you can demonstrate that Uber failed to meet its obligations as a ride-sharing company, such as through inadequate hiring practices or inadequate vehicle safety standards.

Q: What digital evidence can be used to establish the sequence of events leading up to a crash?

A: Investigators can gather evidence from various sources, including vehicle tracking data, digital evidence from the Uber app, surveillance footage, and social media posts.

Q: How can I access the necessary information to establish a breach of duty of care?

A: It is essential to gather evidence through thorough investigations, leveraging digital tools, and analyzing relevant case law and expert testimonies to build a compelling argument.

Q: Are there specific examples of cases where ride-sharing companies have been held liable for a breach of duty of care?

A: Yes, several high-profile cases have highlighted the complexities of ride-sharing liabilities and the need for enhanced safety measures, such as the 2015 Los Angeles ride-sharing crash and the 2016 San Francisco self-driving car accident.
