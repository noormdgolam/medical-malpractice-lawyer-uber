Unreported Injuries and Liability in Ride-Sharing Incidents

When accidents involving Uber or other ride-sharing services occur, identifying and tracking injuries can be a significant challenge. The lack of standardization in passenger documentation often results in voluntary reporting, which frequently falls short of what is needed for effective medical treatment. This oversight can lead to a range of consequences, including:

  • Delayed treatment: Without standard reporting procedures, medical professionals may not receive accurate or timely information about a passenger's condition, leading to delayed treatment.
  • Adequate treatment: Voluntary reporting can result in incomplete or inaccurate information, making it challenging for medical professionals to provide the best possible treatment for a passenger's injuries.
  • Exacerbating patient harm: In cases of hemorrhage or other severe injuries, delayed or inadequate treatment can exacerbate patient harm, leading to long-term consequences for those affected.

This issue has significant implications for Uber's liability in cases where failure to treat hemorrhage or other severe injuries is suspected. If regulatory changes are put in place to hold Uber accountable for adhering to best practices for medical screening and stabilization in the field, it is likely that the company's liability will increase.

Emerging Standards for Medical Response in Transportation Network Company (TNC) Accidents

The transportation sector is actively working towards developing industry-wide guidelines for emergency medical response in TNC accidents. This effort aims to establish a standard framework for ensuring that passengers receive timely and effective medical treatment in the event of an emergency, particularly in cases involving hemorrhage or other life-threatening conditions.

Regulatory changes that could hold Uber accountable for adhering to best practices for medical screening and stabilization in the field may include:

  • Mandatory accident reporting systems: Regulatory bodies may establish mandatory accident reporting systems for ride-sharing services to ensure that accurate and timely information about accidents is recorded and shared.
  • Standardized emergency response protocols: Industry-wide guidelines may be developed to establish standardized emergency response protocols for ride-sharing services, including procedures for medical screening and stabilization.
  • Emergency medical response training: Regulatory bodies may require ride-sharing services to provide emergency medical response training for their drivers, ensuring that they are equipped to handle medical emergencies effectively.

The potential for regulatory changes in this area has far-reaching implications for the transportation sector as a whole. By establishing standard procedures for emergency medical response, the industry can improve passenger safety and reduce the risk of accidents resulting in severe injuries or harm.

Negligence Per Se: The Potential for Uber to be Held Liable for Failure to Treat Hemorrhage

Negligence per se claims against Uber may arise from the company's failure to provide adequate medical resources or timely emergency response in cases of hemorrhage or other life-threatening conditions. This type of claim asserts that the defendant's actions (or inaction) constituted a clear and obvious deviation from a standard of care that resulted in harm to the victim.

To be considered negligence per se, the following elements must be present:

  • A clear and obvious deviation from a standard of care: The defendant's actions must be shown to have deviated significantly from acceptable standards for medical response in emergency situations.
  • A causal link between the deviation and the harm: The defendant's actions must have caused or contributed to the harm suffered by the victim.
  • Damages resulting from the harm: The victim must have suffered actual harm or damage as a direct result of the defendant's actions.

Deliberate actions by Uber to prevent or delay medical treatment in cases of hemorrhage or other life-threatening conditions could lead to findings of negligence per se. This is because the company has a duty to provide a safe and effective medical response to passengers in emergency situations.

In cases where negligence per se claims are filed against Uber, the following factors may be taken into account by the court:

  • The severity of the injuries suffered by the passenger: The court may consider the extent of the harm suffered by the passenger, including any long-term consequences of delayed or inadequate medical treatment.
  • The feasibility of medical treatment: The court may examine whether medical treatment could have been provided in a timely manner, and whether any delays or omissions contributed to the harm suffered by the passenger.
  • The extent of Uber's liability: The court may consider the extent of Uber's responsibility for the harm suffered by the passenger, including any potential liability for failing to provide adequate medical resources or timely emergency response.
