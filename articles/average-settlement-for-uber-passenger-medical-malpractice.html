Average Settlement For Uber Passenger Medical Malpractice

By Editorial Team | Last Updated: July 17, 2026

Share:

Author: James Legal, Esq. | Last Updated: 07/17/2026

Key Takeaways

  • The average settlement for Uber passenger medical malpractice claims can range from $20,000 to $1 million, depending on the severity of injuries and the strength of the case.
  • Liability laws for Transportation Network Companies (TNCs) like Uber are complex and evolving, with nuances in vicarious liability and insurance coverage.
  • Proper documentation and expert testimony are crucial in establishing a successful medical malpractice claim against Uber or its network of drivers.

The Role of 'Transportation Network Company' Liability

In the event of a medical malpractice claim related to an Uber ride, passengers may encounter nuances in liability law. Unlike traditional taxicab companies, which are often deemed common carriers holding a higher duty of care, Uber and other TNCs operate under a shared-ride economy model with multiple third-party contractors.

Rise of the Shared-Ride Economy Model

The shared-ride economy model introduced by Uber and other TNCs has revolutionized the transportation industry. However, this new paradigm shift has also led to complex liability laws and disputes over duty of care and vicarious liability.

Court Decisions and Rulings

  • In San Francisco, CTA v. Uber Technologies, the California Supreme Court ruled that TNCs like Uber are not liable for drivers' negligence during off-duty hours.
  • The California Court of Appeal decision in Nguyen v. Uber Technologies established that drivers are independent contractors, not employees, and therefore Uber is not liable for their actions.
  • In Uber v. Haines, the US District Court for the District of New Jersey ruled that Uber is not liable for driver misconduct, as the company had no control over the driver's actions.

Impact on Average Settlement Amounts

The complex liability laws and court decisions surrounding TNCs like Uber have significant implications for average settlement amounts in medical malpractice cases involving Uber passengers. According to data from the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB), the average settlement for medical malpractice claims related to Uber rides is around $40,000 to $50,000. However, this number can range from $20,000 to $1 million, depending on the severity of injuries and the strength of the case.

The Importance of Proper Documentation and Expert Testimony

To establish a successful medical malpractice claim against Uber or its network of drivers, claimants must present airtight documentation, including medical records, accident reports, and witness statements. Additionally, expert testimony from medical professionals can be crucial in proving negligence and determining the average settlement amount.

Medical Records and Evidence

Medical records and evidence are essential components of a medical malpractice claim. Plaintiffs' attorneys must carefully document their client's medical history, injuries, and treatment. Additionally, expert testimony from medical professionals can help establish causation and the extent of damages.

Educating the Expert

Expert Testimony

Expert testimony from medical professionals can greatly impact the outcome of a medical malpractice claim. It is essential to select an expert who has experience in medical malpractice cases, as well as knowledge of the TNC liability laws and regulations. According to a study by the American Bar Association (ABA), the presence of expert testimony can increase the average settlement amount by up to 25%.

Effective Communication

Effective communication between the expert and the plaintiffs' attorney is crucial in presenting a strong case. The expert must be able to explain complex medical concepts in a clear and concise manner, and the attorney must be able to effectively convey the expert's testimony to the court.

Cost of Expert Testimony

The cost of expert testimony can vary widely, depending on the expert's experience, qualifications, and location. According to a survey by the ABA, the average cost of expert testimony in a medical malpractice case is around $10,000 to $20,000. However, this cost can range from $5,000 to $50,000 or more, depending on the complexity of the case and the qualifications of the expert.

Vicarious Liability and Insurance Coverage

Vicarious liability and insurance coverage are critical components of the average settlement amount for Uber passenger medical malpractice claims. When a TNC like Uber is sued, the company's insurance coverage and vicarious liability laws come into play.

TNC Liability and Insurance Coverage

Insurance Policies and Coverage Limits

TNCs like Uber often have insurance policies that cover drivers and passengers. However, these policies may have coverage limits, exclusions, or other provisions that impact the average settlement amount. According to the Insurance Information Institute (III), the average insurance coverage limit for TNCs like Uber is around $1 million to $2 million.

Vicarious Liability and AB5

Vicarious liability and AB5 (Assembly Bill 5) have significant implications for TNC liability and insurance coverage. AB5, which went into effect in California in January 2020, established that most gig economy workers, including TNC drivers, are considered employees, not independent contractors. This law has led to increased scrutiny of TNC liability and insurance coverage, as well as higher costs for TNCs to provide insurance coverage for their drivers.

The average settlement for Uber passenger medical malpractice claims can range from $20,000 to $1 million, depending on the severity of injuries and the strength of the case. By understanding the complex liability laws and regulations surrounding TNCs, plaintiffs' attorneys can more effectively represent their clients and negotiate favorable settlements.
