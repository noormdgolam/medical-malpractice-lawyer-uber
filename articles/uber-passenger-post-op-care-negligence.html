Uber Passenger Post-Op Care Negligence: A Guide to Navigating Medical Malpractice and Rideshare Liability

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

After a medical procedure, the last thing you need to worry about is navigating the complexities of medical malpractice and rideshare liability. However, if you're an Uber passenger who experienced post-op care negligence, it's essential to take immediate action to protect your rights. Here are the steps to follow in the first 24 hours:

  • Contact your doctor or hospital immediately to report any concerns or complications.
  • Document everything, including dates, times, and details of conversations with medical staff.
  • Take photos or videos of any injuries or symptoms.
  • Contact the rideshare company (Uber) to report the incident and request a copy of the trip record.
  • Consult with a medical malpractice attorney to discuss your options and potential claims.

Evidentiary Requirements for Medical Malpractice Claims

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you'll need to provide evidence that a healthcare provider deviated from the standard of care, causing harm or injury. This may include:

  • Medical records and test results.
  • Expert witness testimony from a qualified healthcare professional.
  • Photographic or video evidence of injuries or symptoms.
  • Witness statements from family members, friends, or medical staff.

Rideshare Liability and Dual-Liability Settlements

Rideshare companies like Uber can be held liable for accidents or injuries that occur during a ride, even if the driver is not at fault. In a dual-liability settlement, both the healthcare provider and the rideshare company may be responsible for damages. Here's a composite case study:

Case Study: Johnson v. Uber and Dr. Smith

A 35-year-old woman, Sarah, underwent a routine surgery at a local hospital. After the procedure, she was discharged and instructed to take a ride home with Uber. However, the driver failed to follow basic safety protocols, causing Sarah to experience a severe allergic reaction during the ride. She was rushed to the emergency room, where she received further treatment. Sarah suffered permanent scarring and emotional distress as a result of the incident.

A medical malpractice attorney represented Sarah in a lawsuit against the hospital and Dr. Smith, the surgeon who performed the procedure. The attorney also filed a claim against Uber for failing to ensure the driver's safety and following proper protocols. The case settled for $750,000, with the hospital and Dr. Smith contributing $300,000 and Uber contributing $450,000.

Research has shown that rideshare companies can be held liable for accidents or injuries that occur during a ride, even if the driver is not at fault.

Conclusion

As an Uber passenger who experienced post-op care negligence, it's essential to take immediate action to protect your rights. By following the steps outlined in this guide and consulting with a medical malpractice attorney, you can navigate the complexities of medical malpractice and rideshare liability and seek the compensation you deserve.
