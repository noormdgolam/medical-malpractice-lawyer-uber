Lyft Passenger Paralysis From Hospital Negligence: A Guide to Seeking Justice

Introduction

As a passenger in a Lyft ride, you expect to arrive safely at your destination. However, when a medical emergency arises, you rely on the nearby hospital to provide timely and quality care. Unfortunately, hospital negligence can lead to devastating consequences, including paralysis. If you or a loved one has suffered paralysis due to hospital negligence while in a Lyft ride, you may be entitled to compensation.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek immediate medical attention: Even if you're already at the hospital, ensure you receive proper care and documentation of your injuries.
  • Document everything: Keep a record of all medical treatments, hospital visits, and communication with healthcare providers.
  • Notify Lyft and the hospital: Inform Lyft of the incident and provide them with any relevant information. Also, notify the hospital's administration and patient safety department.
  • Contact an attorney: Reach out to a medical malpractice attorney experienced in handling cases involving hospital negligence and rideshare companies.
  • Preserve evidence: Do not delete any text messages, emails, or social media posts related to the incident. Also, save any photos or videos taken at the scene.

Evidentiary Requirements

To establish a successful medical malpractice claim against the hospital, you'll need to provide evidence of:

  • Medical negligence
  • Causation (link between the negligence and your injuries)
  • Damages (e.g., medical expenses, lost wages, pain and suffering)

Some key pieces of evidence to gather include:

  • Medical records, including hospital charts and test results
  • Expert witness testimony from medical professionals
  • Witness statements from Lyft drivers, passengers, or hospital staff
  • Photographic and video evidence of the incident

Composite Case Study: Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2020, a 35-year-old woman was rushed to the emergency room after a Lyft ride. While waiting to be treated, she suffered a spinal cord injury due to a hospital staff member's negligence. The hospital failed to properly monitor her vital signs, leading to a delay in treatment. The woman's paralysis was permanent, and she required ongoing medical care and rehabilitation.

Her medical malpractice attorney filed a lawsuit against both the hospital and Lyft, alleging dual liability for the woman's injuries. The case was settled out of court for $5 million, with the hospital paying 80% of the damages and Lyft paying 20%.

This case highlights the importance of holding both rideshare companies and hospitals accountable for their actions. If you or a loved one has suffered a similar injury, don't hesitate to seek justice.
