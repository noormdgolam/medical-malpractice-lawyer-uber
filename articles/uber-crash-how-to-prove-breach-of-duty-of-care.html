Uber Crash: How to Prove Breach of Duty of Care

Introduction

As a rideshare passenger, you trust that the driver will transport you safely to your destination. However, when an Uber crash occurs, it can be a traumatic and life-altering experience. If you've been involved in an Uber crash, you may be entitled to compensation for your injuries and damages. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps to prove breach of duty of care and what to expect in the process.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek Medical Attention Immediately: Even if you don't feel injured, it's essential to get checked out by a medical professional. Some injuries, such as whiplash, may not be immediately apparent.
  • Document the Scene: Take photos and videos of the crash, including the damage to the vehicle and any visible injuries.
  • Get the Driver's Information: Make sure to get the driver's name, contact information, and the make and model of their vehicle.
  • Report the Incident to Uber: Notify Uber of the incident and provide them with the necessary information.
  • Document Your Injuries: Keep a record of your medical treatment, including doctor's visits, hospital stays, and any prescriptions or medications.

Evidentiary Requirements

To prove breach of duty of care, you'll need to gather evidence that demonstrates the driver's negligence or recklessness. This may include:

  • Police Report: Obtain a copy of the police report, which will document the details of the crash.
  • Witness Statements: Collect statements from any witnesses who may have seen the crash.
  • Medical Records: Provide medical records that detail your injuries and treatment.
  • Vehicle Inspection Report: Get a report from a mechanic or inspection service that documents the damage to the vehicle.

Composite Case Study: Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2019, a 25-year-old woman was involved in an Uber crash in Los Angeles. The driver, who was driving under the influence, crashed into a parked car, causing the woman to suffer severe injuries, including a broken back and head trauma. The woman's medical bills exceeded $200,000, and she was unable to work for several months.

The woman's attorney filed a lawsuit against both Uber and the driver, alleging breach of duty of care and negligence. The case was settled for $1.2 million, with Uber paying $800,000 and the driver paying $400,000.

This case highlights the importance of gathering evidence and seeking compensation for your injuries. If you've been involved in an Uber crash, don't hesitate to seek the help of a qualified attorney.

Legal Standards for Duty of Care

What Constitutes a Duty of Care?

Under tort law, a duty of care is a legal obligation imposed on an individual or entity to conform to a standard of reasonable care while performing any acts that could foreseeably harm others. In the rideshare context, Uber, as a platform, and its drivers each owe a duty of care to passengers. This duty includes:

  • Ensuring the driver possesses a valid license and a clean driving record.
  • Maintaining the vehicle in a safe, roadâ??worthy condition.
  • Providing a safe driving environment free from distractions such as mobile devices.
  • Screening drivers for substance abuse and requiring compliance with traffic laws.

Failure to meet any of these obligations can be construed as a breach, especially when it directly leads to an accident.
