Uber Crash Suing The Hospital Board Of Directors: A Comprehensive Guide for Victims

Understanding the Complexities of Dual-Liability Cases

When an Uber crash results in severe injuries or fatalities, victims and their families often face a daunting task: navigating the complex web of liability that involves both the rideshare company and the hospital where they received treatment.

As a seasoned medical malpractice attorney, I've seen firsthand the devastating consequences of medical negligence and the importance of holding both parties accountable for their actions.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice for the First 24 Hours

  • Seek immediate medical attention: Even if you're not sure if you've been injured, it's essential to get checked out by a medical professional as soon as possible.
  • Document everything: Take photos and videos of the accident scene, your injuries, and any property damage. Keep a record of all communication with the hospital, Uber, and any other parties involved.
  • Preserve evidence: Do not delete any text messages, emails, or social media posts that may be relevant to your case. Keep all receipts and records of medical treatment.
  • Contact an attorney: Reach out to a qualified medical malpractice attorney who has experience handling dual-liability cases involving rideshare companies and hospitals.
  • Do not sign anything: Avoid signing any documents or agreements without consulting your attorney first. This includes any releases or settlements offered by Uber or the hospital.

Evidentiary Requirements for Dual-Liability Cases

To succeed in a dual-liability case, you'll need to provide evidence that both Uber and the hospital were negligent and contributed to your injuries.

The evidentiary requirements for medical malpractice cases typically include:

  • Expert testimony: You'll need to hire medical experts who can testify that the hospital's negligence contributed to your injuries.
  • Medical records: You'll need to obtain and review your medical records to identify any errors or omissions that may have contributed to your injuries.
  • Witness statements: You'll need to gather statements from witnesses who can corroborate your account of the accident and any subsequent medical treatment.
  • Financial documentation: You'll need to provide documentation of any financial losses related to your injuries, such as lost wages or medical expenses.

Realistic Composite Case Study: A $1.2 Million Settlement

In 2019, a 35-year-old woman was involved in a fatal Uber crash in Los Angeles. The woman was rushed to a nearby hospital, where she received substandard care that ultimately contributed to her death. The victim's family hired an attorney who filed a dual-liability lawsuit against both Uber and the hospital.

The evidence presented in the case included:

  • Expert testimony from a medical expert who testified that the hospital's negligence contributed to the victim's death.
  • Medical records that showed the hospital's failure to properly diagnose and treat the victim's injuries.
  • Witness statements from the Uber driver and other witnesses who corroborated the victim's account of the accident.
  • Financial documentation that showed the victim's family suffered significant financial losses related to her death.

The case ultimately settled for $1.2 million, with both Uber and the hospital contributing to the settlement.
