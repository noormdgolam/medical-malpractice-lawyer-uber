How Dual Liability Works In Uber Malpractice Cases

As a rideshare passenger, you trust that you will be safely transported from one location to another. However, accidents can still happen, and when they do, it's essential to understand the concept of dual liability in Uber malpractice cases.

What is Dual Liability?

Dual liability refers to the idea that both the Uber driver and the hospital or medical facility where you received treatment can be held responsible for any injuries or damages sustained during the incident.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice for the First 24 Hours

  • Seek immediate medical attention for any injuries sustained during the incident.
  • Document everything, including the accident, your injuries, and any treatment received.
  • Report the incident to Uber and the hospital or medical facility where you received treatment.
  • Take photos and videos of any visible injuries and property damage.
  • Save all medical records, bills, and communication with healthcare providers.
  • Consult with a medical malpractice attorney specializing in Uber cases.

Evidentiary Requirements for Dual Liability Cases

To establish dual liability in an Uber malpractice case, you will need to provide evidence of the following:

  • Proof of the Uber driver's negligence or reckless behavior.
  • Documentation of the hospital or medical facility's failure to provide adequate care or treatment.
  • Medical records and expert testimony to support your claims of injury and damages.
  • Photos and videos of the accident scene and any property damage.
  • Witness statements and testimony from the Uber driver, hospital staff, and any bystanders.

Case Study: A Composite Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

Ms. Johnson, a 35-year-old mother of two, was riding in an Uber on her way home from a night out with friends. The Uber driver, who was speeding and driving recklessly, crashed into a car, causing Ms. Johnson to suffer a severe head injury. She was rushed to the hospital, where she received inadequate care and treatment, leading to further complications and long-term damage.

Ms. Johnson's medical malpractice attorney filed a lawsuit against both the Uber driver and the hospital, alleging dual liability. The case went to trial, and after a lengthy and complex process, the jury awarded Ms. Johnson a $5 million settlement, with $2 million going to the hospital and $3 million to the Uber driver.

Conclusion

If you or a loved one has been injured in an Uber malpractice case, it's essential to understand the concept of dual liability and the evidentiary requirements necessary to establish a successful claim. By taking immediate action, documenting everything, and consulting with a qualified medical malpractice attorney, you can increase your chances of receiving the compensation you deserve.
