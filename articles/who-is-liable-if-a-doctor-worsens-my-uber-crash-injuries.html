Who Is Liable If A Doctor Worsens My Uber Crash Injuries?

Introduction

As a victim of a rideshare accident, you may be entitled to compensation for your injuries. However, if a doctor's negligence worsens your condition, you may have a complex case on your hands. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to take, the evidentiary requirements, and a composite case study to help you navigate this challenging situation.

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

  • Contact your rideshare company's customer service to report the accident and obtain a copy of the incident report.
  • Seek medical attention immediately, even if you feel fine. Delaying medical treatment can worsen your condition and reduce your chances of a successful claim.
  • Document everything: take photos of the accident scene, your injuries, and any property damage. Keep a record of all medical treatment, including doctor's appointments, medications, and hospital stays.
  • Contact an experienced medical malpractice attorney who has handled cases involving rideshare accidents.

Evidentiary Requirements

To establish liability in a medical malpractice case, you must prove that the doctor's negligence caused harm to you. The following elements must be demonstrated:

  • Duty: The doctor had a duty to provide you with a standard level of care.
  • Breach: The doctor breached this duty by failing to meet the standard of care.
  • Causation: The doctor's breach of duty caused harm to you.
  • Damages: You suffered actual harm as a result of the doctor's negligence.

Composite Case Study

Meet Jane Doe, a 35-year-old mother of two who was involved in a rideshare accident while on her way home from work. She suffered minor injuries, including whiplash and a concussion. However, her treating physician, Dr. Smith, failed to properly diagnose and treat her injuries, leading to further complications, including a herniated disk and chronic pain.

Jane's medical bills exceeded $100,000, and she was unable to work for several months. She hired an experienced medical malpractice attorney who filed a lawsuit against Dr. Smith and the hospital where she received treatment. The case went to trial, and the jury awarded Jane $750,000 in damages, including $500,000 for past medical expenses and $250,000 for pain and suffering.

The rideshare company was also sued for their role in the accident, and they settled for $200,000.

Realistic Settlement Example

According to the American Medical Association (AMA), the average medical malpractice settlement is around $250,000. However, this amount can vary greatly depending on the severity of the injuries, the extent of the damages, and the jurisdiction in which the case is heard.

For example, a study published by the Journal of the American Medical Association (JAMA) found that the median award for medical malpractice cases involving severe injuries was around $400,000.

It's essential to note that these figures are only averages, and your case may be worth significantly more or less depending on the specific circumstances.

Conclusion

If a doctor's negligence worsens your Uber crash injuries, you may be entitled to compensation. By following the steps outlined in this guide, documenting everything, and hiring an experienced medical malpractice attorney, you can navigate this complex situation and obtain the justice you deserve.
