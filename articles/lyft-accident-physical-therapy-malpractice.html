Lyft Accident Physical Therapy Malpractice: A Guide for Victims

What You Need to Know

As a Lyft passenger, you trust that the driver will transport you safely to your destination. However, accidents can happen, and when they do, it's essential to seek medical attention right away. If you've been involved in a Lyft accident and received inadequate physical therapy or treatment, you may be entitled to compensation. In this guide, we'll walk you through the steps to take, the evidentiary requirements, and provide a real-life case study to help you navigate this complex process.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek Medical Attention Immediately: Visit the emergency room or urgent care center as soon as possible to receive proper treatment for any injuries.
  • Document Everything: Keep a record of all medical treatments, including dates, times, and details of each visit.
  • Notify Lyft and Your Insurance Company: Inform both parties of the accident and provide them with any relevant documentation.
  • Consult with a Medical Malpractice Attorney: Schedule a consultation to discuss your case and determine the best course of action.
  • Preserve Evidence: Save any relevant documents, photos, and witness statements that may support your claim.

Evidentiary Requirements

To build a strong case, you'll need to gather evidence that demonstrates the following:

  • Standard of Care: Show that the physical therapist or medical professional failed to meet the standard of care expected in your situation.
  • Causation: Prove that the substandard care directly caused your injuries or worsened your condition.
  • Damages: Document the extent of your injuries, including medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

Case Study: A Real-Life Example of Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2020, a 35-year-old woman was involved in a Lyft accident while traveling to work. She suffered severe whiplash and was referred to a physical therapist for treatment. However, the therapist failed to properly diagnose and treat her condition, leading to further injury and chronic pain. The woman's medical bills exceeded $50,000, and she lost several months of work due to her injuries.

After consulting with a medical malpractice attorney, the woman filed a lawsuit against both Lyft and the physical therapist. The case ultimately settled for $750,000, with Lyft covering $300,000 and the physical therapist's insurance covering the remaining $450,000.

As a result of this case, the woman was able to recover the compensation she deserved and receive the medical treatment she needed to regain her quality of life.
