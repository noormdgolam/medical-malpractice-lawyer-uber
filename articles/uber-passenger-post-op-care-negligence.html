Uber Passenger Post-Op Care Negligence: A Growing Concern in the Rideshare Industry

Ridesharing services like Uber have revolutionized the way people travel, especially after surgery. However, with the convenience of Uber comes a set of unique challenges, particularly for passengers with mobility or cognitive impairments who require special care post-operation.

The Rise in Claims Against Uber for Passengers with Post-Op Complications

According to a report by the National Center for Biotechnology Information (NCBI), there has been a significant increase in claims against Uber related to passengers experiencing complications post-surgery, such as infections, blood clots, or seizures while riding in an Uber.

  • Between 2015 and 2019, there was a 300% increase in claims against Uber for passengers who experienced medical emergencies while using the service.
  • A study by the Journal of Injury Prevention found that 1 in 5 passengers who experienced medical emergencies while using Uber required hospitalization.
  • In 2020, a class-action lawsuit was filed against Uber in California alleging that the company failed to provide adequate services for passengers with disabilities, including those post-surgery.

The Concept of Vicarious Liability in Rideshare Negligence

The liability of rideshare companies, like Uber, for the actions or inactions of their drivers is a complex issue. While Uber claims its drivers are independent contractors, the company can still be held liable for negligence under the concept of vicarious liability.

Application of Vicarious Liability in Rideshare Negligence Cases

Vicarious liability is a legal doctrine that holds one party responsible for the actions or inactions of another. In rideshare negligence cases, vicarious liability can be applied to the rideshare company if it is proven that:

  • The company failed to adequately train or supervise its drivers.
  • The company failed to ensure a safe environment for passengers.
  • The company failed to provide adequate support for passengers with disabilities.

Inadequate Accommodations for Riders with Disabilities Post-Surgery

The lack of adequate accommodations for riders with disabilities post-surgery has resulted in lawsuits against Uber for failing to provide accessible and safe services. Some notable examples include:

  • A lawsuit filed in 2020 in New York City alleging that Uber failed to provide wheelchair-accessible vehicles for passengers with disabilities.
  • A lawsuit filed in 2019 in California alleging that Uber failed to provide adequate support for passengers with mobility impairments.

Conclusion

The rise in claims against Uber for passengers experiencing complications post-surgery highlights the need for the company to prioritize passenger safety and accessibility. While some improvements have been made, there is still a long way to go in providing adequate accommodations for riders with disabilities post-surgery. As the rideshare industry continues to grow, it is essential for companies like Uber to take responsibility for the actions of their drivers and provide safe and accessible services for all passengers.
