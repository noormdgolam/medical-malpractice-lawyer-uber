Lyft Accident Doctor Failing To Diagnose Concussion: A Guide for Victims

Understanding Your Rights and Options

As a rideshare passenger, you trust that the driver and medical professionals will prioritize your safety and well-being. However, when a doctor fails to diagnose a concussion after a Lyft accident, you may be entitled to compensation for your injuries and suffering.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek immediate medical attention at a hospital or urgent care center for a thorough examination and diagnosis.
  • Document all medical records, including test results, diagnoses, and treatment plans.
  • Notify Lyft's customer service department about the accident and your injuries.
  • Consult with a medical malpractice attorney experienced in handling rideshare accident cases.
  • Preserve all evidence, including photos, videos, and witness statements.

Evidentiary Requirements for a Medical Malpractice Claim

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you must demonstrate that the doctor's failure to diagnose the concussion was a deviation from the standard of care, resulting in harm or injury.

  • Expert testimony from a medical professional who can attest to the standard of care and the doctor's deviation from it.
  • Medical records and test results that confirm the doctor's failure to diagnose the concussion.
  • Documentation of your injuries and treatment, including any subsequent complications or worsening of symptoms.
  • Witness statements or testimony from other medical professionals who can corroborate your account.

Composite Case Study: A $1.2 Million Settlement for a Rideshare Passenger

Ms. Johnson was a 35-year-old passenger in a Lyft ride when the driver lost control of the vehicle, causing a multi-car pileup. Ms. Johnson suffered a severe concussion, which was not properly diagnosed by the emergency room doctor. As a result, her condition worsened, and she experienced severe headaches, memory loss, and depression.

Ms. Johnson consulted with a medical malpractice attorney, who filed a lawsuit against the doctor and the hospital. After a lengthy investigation and negotiations, the parties reached a settlement of $1.2 million, which covered Ms. Johnson's medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

According to the American Medical Association (AMA) Code of Medical Ethics, doctors have a duty to provide accurate diagnoses and treatment plans. In this case, the doctor's failure to diagnose Ms. Johnson's concussion was a clear deviation from the standard of care, resulting in harm and injury to the patient.
