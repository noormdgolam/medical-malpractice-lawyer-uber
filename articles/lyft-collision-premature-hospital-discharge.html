Lyft Collision Premature Hospital Discharge: A Guide to Seeking Justice

Understanding Your Rights and Options

As a victim of a Lyft collision who was prematurely discharged from the hospital, you may be entitled to compensation for your injuries and related expenses. Actionable Steps to Take Within the First 24 Hours

  • Document all communication with medical professionals, including dates, times, and details of conversations.
  • Gather contact information for any witnesses, including Lyft drivers or passengers.
  • Take photographs of any injuries or property damage.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney who specializes in personal injury cases.

Time is of the essence. Within the first 24 hours, the medical record begins to form, and any gaps or inconsistencies become easier to pinpoint.   • Medical records, including hospital discharge papers and any subsequent treatment.
  • Police reports or incident statements from Lyft and law enforcement.

Lyft may be held liable for the collision, as well as the premature discharge from the hospital. To establish liability, you'll need to prove that Lyft's negligence or recklessness contributed to the collision and subsequent medical malpractice.
