Legal Framework: How Liability is Determined

Liability in a rideshare‚??related medical malpractice claim typically hinges on two legal doctrines: negligence and vicarious liability. Negligence requires proving that a duty of care existed, that the duty was breached, and that the breach caused the injury. In the context of Uber, the platform can be held liable if it failed to adequately screen drivers, ignored repeated safety complaints, or did not enforce its own safety policies.

Vicarious liability, on the other hand, can attach to the driver for actions taken while operating the vehicle. If a driver‚??s reckless driving caused a sudden jolt that aggravated a pre‚??existing spinal condition, the driver (and potentially Uber as the employer) may be responsible for the ensuing medical complications.

When a hospital or surgeon is added to the equation, the plaintiff must demonstrate that the medical provider deviated from accepted standards‚??such as performing an unnecessary procedure, misreading imaging, or failing to monitor postoperative complications. Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Pre‚??existing conditions do not automatically bar recovery. If the accident aggravated the condition or if medical negligence worsened it, you may still recover damages.
