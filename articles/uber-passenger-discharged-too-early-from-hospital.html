Uber Passenger Discharged Too Early From Hospital: A Guide to Medical Malpractice and Rideshare Liability

Introduction

As an Uber passenger, you expect a safe and reliable ride to your destination. However, when you're taken to the hospital after an accident, you also expect to receive proper medical care. Unfortunately, medical malpractice can occur even in the best hospitals, and in some cases, the hospital's negligence can be compounded by the actions of the rideshare company. In this article, we'll guide you through the steps to take if you've been discharged too early from the hospital as an Uber passenger.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek Immediate Medical Attention: If you're experiencing any symptoms or discomfort after the accident, seek medical attention right away. Even if you're feeling fine, it's essential to get checked out by a medical professional.
  • Document Everything: Keep a record of all your medical treatment, including dates, times, and details of what happened. Also, document any conversations you have with medical staff or Uber representatives.
  • Report the Incident to Uber: Inform Uber about the accident and your discharge from the hospital. They may have a process in place for reporting incidents, so be sure to follow their procedures.
  • Consult with a Medical Malpractice Attorney: Reach out to a qualified medical malpractice attorney who can help you navigate the complex process of filing a claim. They can also advise you on the evidentiary requirements and potential compensation.
  • Preserve Evidence: Keep any medical records, police reports, and other relevant documents in a safe place. Your attorney may need these to build a strong case.

Evidentiary Requirements

The evidentiary requirements for a medical malpractice claim can vary depending on the jurisdiction and the specific circumstances of your case. However, some common requirements include:

  • Medical Expert Testimony: You'll need to hire a medical expert who can testify that the hospital's negligence caused your injuries or worsened your condition.
  • Medical Records: Your attorney will need to review your medical records to identify any potential errors or omissions in your care.
  • Witness Statements: Your attorney may need to interview witnesses, including medical staff, Uber drivers, or passengers, to gather more information about the incident.
  • Police Reports: If the accident was reported to the police, your attorney may need a copy of the police report to document the incident.

Composite Case Study: Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

Case Summary

In 2020, a 32-year-old Uber passenger was involved in a car accident while traveling to a friend's house. She was taken to the hospital, where she was discharged too early with a misdiagnosis. As a result, her injuries worsened, and she required additional surgery. She filed a lawsuit against both the hospital and Uber, alleging medical malpractice and negligence.

The case settled for $1.2 million, with the hospital paying $800,000 and Uber paying $400,000. The settlement was reached after a lengthy negotiation process, during which the plaintiff's attorney presented evidence of the hospital's negligence and Uber's failure to ensure their passenger's safety.
