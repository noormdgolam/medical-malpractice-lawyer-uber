Uber Crash Doctor Failed To Consult Specialist: A Guide to Medical Malpractice and Dual Liability

What You Need to Know

As an Uber passenger, you trust the driver to get you safely to your destination. But what happens when an accident occurs and the driver is at fault? Not only can you be injured, but you may also be forced to deal with medical malpractice by the doctor who treated you. In this guide, we will walk you through the steps to take when an Uber crash doctor fails to consult a specialist, and what you can do to protect your rights.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek immediate medical attention: Even if you don't feel any pain or symptoms, it's essential to get checked out by a doctor as soon as possible. Delaying medical attention can lead to further complications and reduce your chances of a successful claim.
  • Document everything: Take photos of the accident scene, your injuries, and any property damage. Also, keep a record of all medical treatment, including doctor's visits, hospital stays, and medication.
  • Report the accident to Uber: File a report with Uber's customer service department, providing as much detail as possible about the accident.
  • Consult with an attorney: Reach out to a medical malpractice attorney who has experience handling cases involving Uber accidents and medical negligence.

Evidentiary Requirements

In order to prove medical malpractice, you must demonstrate that the doctor's failure to consult a specialist was a deviation from the standard of care, and that this deviation caused you harm. The following evidence may be required:

  • Medical records: Your complete medical history, including diagnoses, treatments, and test results.
  • Expert testimony: A specialist in the relevant field will be needed to testify that the doctor's actions (or lack thereof) fell short of the standard of care.
  • Photographic and video evidence: Images and videos of the accident scene, your injuries, and any property damage.
  • Witness statements: Statements from witnesses, including the Uber driver, passengers, and any bystanders.

Case Study: A Composite Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2020, a 30-year-old woman was involved in a head-on collision while riding in an Uber. She suffered severe injuries, including a broken neck and spinal cord damage. The Uber driver was at fault, and the woman was rushed to a nearby hospital for treatment.

However, the emergency room doctor failed to consult a specialist, opting instead to treat the woman with pain medication and a cervical collar. This decision led to further complications, including a spinal infection and permanent nerve damage.

The woman's family hired a medical malpractice attorney, who filed a lawsuit against both Uber and the hospital. The case settled for $5 million, with $2 million allocated to the woman's medical expenses and $3 million for pain and suffering.

Conclusion

If you've been involved in an Uber crash and suffered medical malpractice, you have the right to seek compensation for your injuries. By following the steps outlined in this guide and consulting with an experienced attorney, you can protect your rights and hold both Uber and the hospital accountable for their negligence.
