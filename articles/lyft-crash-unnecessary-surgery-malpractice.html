Lyft Crash Unnecessary Surgery Malpractice: A Comprehensive Guide for Victims

Introduction

As a medical malpractice attorney, I have seen firsthand the devastating consequences of unnecessary surgeries performed on victims of Lyft crashes. If you or a loved one has been in a Lyft accident and subsequently underwent an unnecessary surgical procedure, you may be entitled to compensation for your medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek immediate medical attention for any injuries sustained in the Lyft crash.

  • Document all medical records, including test results, diagnoses, and treatment plans.

  • Report the incident to Lyft and the police.

  • Consult with a medical malpractice attorney to discuss your case and potential options for seeking compensation.

  • Do not sign any medical releases or settlement agreements without consulting with an attorney.

Evidentiary Requirements

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you must prove that the healthcare provider deviated from the standard of care and that this deviation caused your injuries. In the context of a Lyft crash, this may involve:

  • Expert testimony from a medical professional who can testify that the surgery was unnecessary.

  • Medical records and test results that demonstrate the healthcare provider's deviation from the standard of care.

  • Documentation of the healthcare provider's communication with you, including any warnings or explanations regarding the risks and benefits of the surgery.

Case Study: A Composite Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

A 32-year-old woman was involved in a Lyft crash and suffered a minor injury to her knee. Despite her symptoms, the emergency room physician ordered an MRI, which revealed a minor tear in her meniscus. The physician recommended arthroscopic surgery to repair the tear, which the woman consented to. However, the surgery was unnecessary, and the tear would have healed on its own with physical therapy.

The woman hired a medical malpractice attorney, who discovered that the emergency room physician had deviated from the standard of care by ordering the MRI and recommending surgery. The attorney also discovered that Lyft had failed to properly investigate the crash scene and had not reported the incident to the police in a timely manner.

The woman's attorney filed a lawsuit against both the hospital and Lyft, alleging dual liability for the unnecessary surgery and the subsequent medical expenses. The case settled for $750,000, with $500,000 allocated to the woman's medical expenses and $250,000 allocated to her pain and suffering.

Understanding Medical Malpractice in Rideshare Accidents

Rideshare platforms such as Lyft create a unique legal landscape because the liability can be split between the driver, the rideshare company, and any medical providers who treat the injured passenger. When a surgery is deemed unnecessary, the malpractice claim typically targets the physician or hospital, but the rideshare company may also be implicated if its negligence contributed to the injury or delayed proper medical assessment.

Preserving Evidence and Building a Strong Case

Effective evidence preservation begins at the crash scene and continues through the entire medical treatment timeline. Below are practical steps to safeguard your claim:

  1. Photograph the crash scene: Capture vehicle positions, road conditions, and any visible injuries.
  2. Obtain a copy of the police report: This official document often contains crucial details about fault and injury severity.
  3. Secure all medical records: Request complete records from every provider, including ER notes, imaging studies, and operative reports.
  4. Maintain a symptom diary: Record pain levels, mobility limitations, and any changes in condition over time.
  5. Collect witness statements: Passengers, drivers, or bystanders can corroborate your account of the crash and initial injuries.

These records become the backbone of expert analysis, allowing a medical professional to pinpoint where the standard of care was breached.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I sue Lyft and the hospital in the same lawsuit?

Yes. Because the injuries stem from a single incident, a plaintiff can assert claims against both the rideshare company (for negligent operation) and the medical provider (for malpractice). Coordinating the claims often results in a more efficient resolution and can increase total recovery.

What if I signed a waiver after the surgery?

Waivers signed after the fact do not automatically bar a malpractice claim. If the waiver was obtained under duress, without full disclosure, or after the surgery was performed, it may be invalid. An attorney can assess its enforceability.

How long does a typical case take to settle?

Cases involving dual liability usually settle within 12â??18 months, though complex cases that proceed to trial can extend beyond two years. Early evidence collection and expert involvement often accelerate settlement negotiations.
