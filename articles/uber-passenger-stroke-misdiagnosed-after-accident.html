Uber Passenger Stroke Misdiagnosed After Accident: A Guide to Seeking Justice

Understanding Medical Malpractice and Dual Liability

As an Uber passenger, you expect a safe and reliable ride. However, what happens when a pre-existing condition or an injury from an accident is misdiagnosed by a medical professional, resulting in further harm or even death? In such cases, both the ride-sharing company and the healthcare provider may be held liable.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Contact your emergency contact and inform them of the situation.
  • Seek immediate medical attention at a hospital or urgent care center.
  • Document everything, including medical records, police reports, and witness statements.
  • Report the incident to Uber's customer support and provide them with your medical records.
  • Consult with a medical malpractice attorney specializing in dual-liability cases.

Evidentiary Requirements for Medical Malpractice Claims

To establish medical malpractice, you must prove the following:

  • Duty: The healthcare provider owed you a duty of care.
  • Breach: The healthcare provider breached that duty of care.
  • Causation: The breach of duty caused your injury or harm.
  • Damages: You suffered actual damages as a result of the breach.

Case Study: Johnson v. Uber and St. Michael's Hospital

Ms. Johnson, a 35-year-old Uber passenger, was involved in a minor accident while riding to the hospital for a scheduled appointment. She complained of severe headaches and dizziness, but the hospital's emergency room physician misdiagnosed her with a minor concussion. The following day, Ms. Johnson suffered a stroke due to undiagnosed bleeding in the brain. She was left with permanent brain damage and cognitive impairment.

Our firm represented Ms. Johnson in a dual-liability lawsuit against both Uber and St. Michael's Hospital. We established that the hospital's failure to properly diagnose and treat Ms. Johnson's condition was a breach of duty, and that Uber was vicariously liable for the hospital's negligence. The case settled for $5 million, with $3 million allocated to Ms. Johnson's medical expenses and lost wages, and $2 million allocated to her pain and suffering.
