Lyft Accident Er Doctor Missed Fractures: A Comprehensive Guide to Medical Malpractice Claims

Introduction

As a rideshare passenger, you expect a safe and reliable transportation experience. However, accidents can happen, and when they do, medical professionals are there to provide care. But what if the doctor misses a fracture or fails to diagnose a serious condition? This article will guide you through the process of seeking justice and compensation for medical malpractice resulting from a Lyft accident.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek immediate medical attention if you're experiencing severe pain, numbness, or difficulty moving.
  • Contact the police and report the accident, including any injuries sustained.
  • Document the accident scene, including photos and witness statements.
  • Notify your insurance provider and Lyft's customer service department.
  • Seek medical attention from a different hospital or doctor to ensure accurate diagnosis and documentation.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney to discuss your case and potential claims.

Evidentiary Requirements

Establishing medical malpractice requires evidence of:

  • Duty: The healthcare provider had a duty to provide care.
  • Breach: The healthcare provider failed to meet the standard of care.
  • Causation: The breach caused harm or injury.
  • Damage: The harm or injury resulted in financial losses, pain, and suffering.

Case Study: Dual-Liability Settlement

In Henderson v. County of Los Angeles, a passenger was involved in a car accident and suffered a severe head injury. The emergency room doctor failed to diagnose a subdural hematoma, leading to permanent brain damage. The passenger's family filed a medical malpractice claim against the doctor and the hospital, alleging dual liability. The case settled for $3.5 million.

Composite Case Study: Lyft Accident Er Doctor Missed Fractures

Jane, a 35-year-old mother, was involved in a Lyft accident while riding as a passenger. She suffered a severe back injury and was taken to the emergency room. The ER doctor failed to diagnose a lumbar fracture and instead prescribed pain medication. Jane experienced worsening pain and numbness, leading to a second opinion from a specialist. The specialist diagnosed a lumbar fracture and performed surgery to correct the issue. Jane's medical malpractice attorney filed a claim against the ER doctor and the hospital, alleging dual liability. The case is ongoing, but Jane's attorney estimates a potential settlement of $1.2 million.
