Uber Crash Pediatric Malpractice In The ER: A Comprehensive Guide

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

After an Uber crash involving a pediatric patient, it is crucial to act quickly to ensure the best possible outcome. Here are the steps to take in the first 24 hours:

  • Contact the police and report the incident immediately.
  • Seek medical attention for the pediatric patient at the nearest hospital or emergency room.
  • Notify Uber's customer service and report the incident.
  • Document everything, including witness statements, photographs, and any communication with the hospital or medical staff.
  • Do not sign any medical releases or consent forms without consulting an attorney first.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney who specializes in pediatric cases.

Evidentiary Requirements

In a medical malpractice case involving a pediatric patient, the following evidence may be required:

  • Medical records, including the patient's medical history, treatment, and any complications.
  • Witness statements from hospital staff, medical professionals, and any witnesses to the accident.
  • Photographs and videos of the accident scene and any injuries sustained by the pediatric patient.
  • Expert testimony from medical professionals, including pediatricians and emergency room physicians.

It is essential to work with an experienced medical malpractice attorney to gather and preserve this evidence, as it can be crucial in building a strong case.

Composite Case Study: A Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In a recent case, a 3-year-old child was involved in an Uber crash and was rushed to the emergency room with severe head injuries. The hospital's emergency room staff failed to properly diagnose and treat the child's condition, leading to further complications and long-term brain damage.

The family of the child hired a medical malpractice attorney who specialized in pediatric cases. After conducting a thorough investigation, the attorney discovered that the hospital had a history of similar cases involving delayed diagnoses and inadequate treatment.

The attorney filed a lawsuit against both Uber and the hospital, alleging dual liability for the child's injuries. The case went to trial, and the jury awarded the family a settlement of $10 million.

This case highlights the importance of acting quickly and seeking the advice of an experienced medical malpractice attorney in cases involving pediatric patients and rideshare accidents.
