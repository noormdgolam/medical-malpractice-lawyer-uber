Lyft Collision Medical Malpractice: A Guide for Victims

Understanding the Dual-Liability of Rideshare and Hospital Negligence

As a medical malpractice attorney, I have witnessed firsthand the devastating consequences of Lyft collisions and subsequent medical negligence. When a rideshare driver is involved in an accident, the situation can become even more complicated due to the dual-liability of both the driver and the hospital. In this guide, we will walk you through the evidentiary requirements, actionable step-by-step advice, and a realistic composite case study to help you navigate this complex process.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice for the First 24 Hours

  • Seek Medical Attention Immediately: If you or a loved one has been involved in a Lyft collision, it is crucial to seek medical attention right away. This will not only ensure your health and well-being but also create a paper trail that can be used as evidence in your case.
  • Document Everything: Keep a record of all medical treatment, including dates, times, and descriptions of your injuries. Also, document any communication with the hospital, insurance companies, and rideshare services.
  • Preserve Evidence: If possible, take photos of the accident scene, injuries, and any property damage. Also, save any relevant communication, such as text messages or emails.
  • Consult with an Attorney: Reach out to a medical malpractice attorney who has experience handling dual-liability cases. They will guide you through the process and help you navigate the complexities of your case.

Evidentiary Requirements for Medical Malpractice Cases

To establish medical malpractice, you must demonstrate that the hospital or healthcare provider deviated from the standard of care, resulting in harm or injury. The following are essential elements to prove:

  • Standard of Care: Establish the standard of care that should have been provided by the hospital or healthcare provider.
  • Deviation from Standard of Care: Show that the hospital or healthcare provider deviated from the standard of care, resulting in harm or injury.
  • Causation: Prove that the deviation from the standard of care caused the harm or injury.
  • Damages: Establish the extent of the damages, including medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

Composite Case Study: Lyft Collision Medical Malpractice Settlement

A 35-year-old woman, Sarah, was involved in a Lyft collision while riding as a passenger. She suffered severe injuries, including a broken femur and a traumatic brain injury. After being taken to a nearby hospital, Sarah received subpar treatment, including delayed diagnosis and inadequate pain management. As a result, her condition worsened, leading to further complications.

Sarah's medical malpractice attorney, with expertise in dual-liability cases, took on her case. After a thorough investigation, they established that the hospital deviated from the standard of care, resulting in harm and injury to Sarah. The attorney negotiated a settlement with both the hospital and Lyft, securing a total of $1.5 million for Sarah's medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

According to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA), there were over 6,000 rideshare-related crashes in the United States in 2020, resulting in over 3,000 injuries and 100 fatalities. The American Medical Association (AMA) estimates that medical errors are the third leading cause of death in the United States, resulting in over 250,000 deaths annually.
