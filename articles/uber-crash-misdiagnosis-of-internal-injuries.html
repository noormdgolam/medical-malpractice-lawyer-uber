Uber Crash Misdiagnosis of Internal Injuries: A Comprehensive Guide for Victims

Understanding the Risks and Consequences of Misdiagnosis

Misdiagnosis of internal injuries in the aftermath of an Uber crash can have devastating consequences. When a driver or passenger is involved in a collision, they may experience severe internal injuries that can lead to long-term health complications or even death if left untreated. Unfortunately, medical professionals may misdiagnose or delay diagnosis, exacerbating the situation.

Actionable Steps to Take in the First 24 Hours

  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney immediately to discuss your case and options.
  • Seek immediate medical attention at a hospital or urgent care center, even if you feel fine.
  • Request a copy of your medical records, including test results and diagnosis, from the treating physician.
  • Document all communication with medical professionals, including dates, times, and details of conversations.
  • Keep a record of all medical expenses, including hospital stays, medication, and follow-up appointments.

Evidentiary Requirements for a Medical Malpractice Claim

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you must demonstrate that a healthcare provider deviated from the standard of care, resulting in harm or injury. The following evidence is typically required:

  • Expert testimony from a qualified medical professional who can attest to the substandard care.
  • Medical records, including test results, diagnosis, and treatment plans.
  • Witness statements from family members, friends, or medical staff who observed the care provided.
  • Documentation of any changes in treatment or diagnosis that may indicate a misdiagnosis.

Case Study: A Composite Example of a Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2019, a 35-year-old Uber driver was involved in a high-speed collision while transporting a passenger. The passenger suffered severe internal injuries, including a ruptured spleen and liver damage. Despite seeking immediate medical attention, the passenger was misdiagnosed with minor injuries and released from the hospital after a few hours. The passenger suffered significant complications, including sepsis and organ failure, which required multiple surgeries and a prolonged hospital stay.

After consulting with a medical malpractice attorney, the passenger filed a lawsuit against the hospital and Uber, alleging dual liability for the misdiagnosis and subsequent harm. The case was settled out of court for $2.5 million, with the hospital contributing 70% and Uber contributing 30%.
