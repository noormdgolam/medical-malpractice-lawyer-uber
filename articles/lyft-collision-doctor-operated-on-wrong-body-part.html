Evidentiary Requirements for Medical Malpractice Claims

To prove medical malpractice, you must demonstrate that the healthcare provider deviated from the standard of care and that this deviation caused your injuries. This typically requires:

  • A medical expert's opinion that the healthcare provider breached the standard of care.
  • Documentation of the healthcare provider's mistake, such as medical records or witness statements.
  • Proof of causation, linking the healthcare provider's mistake to your injuries.

Beyond the basic elements, successful plaintiffs often present additional corroborating evidence: operative notes that show a mismatch between the intended surgical site and the actual incision, imaging studies (e.g., CT or MRI) performed before and after the procedure, and testimony from nursing staff who observed the error in real time. Preserving Evidence After a Medical Error

Time is of the essence when preserving evidence. Hospitals often retain records for a limited period, and electronic health records (EHRs) can be altered or overwritten. Victims should immediately request copies of all medical documents, including:

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

Can I sue Lyft and the hospital in the same lawsuit?

Yes. Because the claims arise from distinct legal duties‚??Lyft‚??s duty to provide safe transportation and the hospital‚??s duty to deliver competent medical care‚??both defendants can be named in a single complaint. What if I signed a waiver after the accident?

Signing a waiver does not automatically bar you from pursuing a malpractice claim. Waivers typically cover the rideshare service‚??s liability for the accident itself, not the subsequent medical treatment. An attorney will review the language of any waiver to determine its impact on your case.

How long do I have to file a claim?

Statutes of limitations vary by state but generally range from one to three years for medical malpractice and two to four years for personal injury claims against rideshare companies. Prompt action is essential to preserve evidence and avoid missing critical filing deadlines.

Will my insurance premiums increase?

Filing a claim does not directly affect your personal auto insurance premiums, as rideshare companies carry their own liability coverage. However, if you pursue a personal injury claim against a driver who was not a Lyft employee, that driver‚??s insurance may be impacted.
