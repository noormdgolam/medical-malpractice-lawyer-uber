Lyft Accident Spinal Cord Injury Negligence: A Guide for Victims

Understanding Your Rights and Options

As a victim of a Lyft accident resulting in a spinal cord injury, you may be facing a long and challenging road to recovery. Not only are you dealing with the physical and emotional trauma of your injury, but you also need to navigate the complex legal system to ensure that you receive the compensation and support you need.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice for the First 24 Hours

  • Seek Medical Attention Immediately: Get to a hospital or medical facility as soon as possible to receive proper treatment for your injuries.
  • Document Everything: Take photos and videos of the accident scene, your injuries, and any property damage. Also, keep a record of any medical treatment you receive, including doctor's visits, hospital stays, and medication.
  • Report the Accident to Lyft: Inform Lyft of the accident and provide them with your contact information and any relevant details about the incident.
  • Consult with an Attorney: Reach out to a qualified medical malpractice attorney who has experience handling Lyft accidents and spinal cord injury cases. They can help you understand your rights and options, and guide you through the legal process.
  • Preserve Evidence: Do not delete any records, messages, or emails related to the accident. Also, do not post anything on social media that could be used against you in court.

Evidentiary Requirements for a Successful Claim

To build a strong case, you will need to gather and present evidence that proves the following:

  • Negligence: Show that Lyft or the driver was negligent in some way, such as failing to follow safety protocols or driving recklessly.
  • Causation: Demonstrate that the Lyft driver's negligence caused your spinal cord injury.
  • Damages: Provide evidence of the extent of your injuries and the financial impact they have had on your life.

Case Study: A Composite Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2019, a 35-year-old woman was a passenger in a Lyft vehicle when it was involved in a collision. She suffered a severe spinal cord injury and was rushed to the hospital. Due to the hospital's negligence, her condition worsened, and she required additional surgery. The case was settled for $3.5 million, with $2.5 million coming from Lyft and $1 million from the hospital.

Key evidence in this case included:

  • Surveillance footage of the accident
  • Medical records and expert testimony
  • Witness statements and Lyft's internal investigation
