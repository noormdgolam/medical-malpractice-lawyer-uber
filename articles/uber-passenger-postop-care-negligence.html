Uber Passenger Post-Op Care Negligence: A Comprehensive Guide to Pursuing Justice

Understanding the Risks of Post-Op Care Negligence

As an Uber passenger, you may not expect to face medical malpractice after a routine ride. However, post-op care negligence can occur when healthcare providers fail to provide adequate care, leading to serious complications and even death. If you or a loved one has experienced post-op care negligence after an Uber ride, it's essential to understand your rights and the steps you can take to pursue justice.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek immediate medical attention: If you're experiencing any symptoms or complications after your Uber ride, seek medical attention right away.
  • Document everything: Keep a record of your medical treatment, including dates, times, and details of what happened.
  • Preserve evidence: Save any relevant medical records, test results, and communication with healthcare providers.
  • Report the incident to Uber: Inform Uber's customer service department about the incident and provide them with any relevant information.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney: Reach out to a qualified medical malpractice attorney who can guide you through the process and help you navigate the complexities of your case.

Evidentiary Requirements

To pursue a medical malpractice claim, you'll need to establish that the healthcare provider breached the standard of care and that this breach caused your injuries. Some key evidentiary requirements include:

  • Expert testimony: You'll need to hire a medical expert who can testify that the healthcare provider deviated from the standard of care.
  • Medical records: You'll need to obtain and preserve your medical records, including test results, diagnoses, and treatment plans.
  • Witness statements: You may need to gather statements from witnesses, including family members, friends, or other healthcare providers.
  • Photographic and video evidence: You may need to collect photographic or video evidence of your injuries or any relevant medical equipment.

Realistic Composite Case Study

Meet Jane Doe, a 35-year-old woman who underwent a routine knee replacement surgery at a local hospital after a car accident. After the surgery, she was discharged and instructed to take Uber home. However, during the ride, she began to experience severe pain and shortness of breath. She was rushed to the emergency room, where she was diagnosed with a pulmonary embolism (blood clot in the lung) caused by the hospital's failure to administer proper blood thinners post-surgery.

Jane's family hired a medical malpractice attorney who successfully negotiated a settlement with both the hospital and Uber. The total settlement amount was $1.2 million, with $800,000 coming from the hospital and $400,000 from Uber.
