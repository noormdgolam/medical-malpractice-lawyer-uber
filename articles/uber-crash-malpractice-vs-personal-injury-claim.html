Uber Crash Malpractice Vs Personal Injury Claim

By Editorial Team | Last Updated: July 17, 2026

Uber Crash Malpractice vs Personal Injury Claim: A Comprehensive Guide

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

After an Uber crash, it's essential to act quickly to ensure your rights are protected. Here are the steps to take in the first 24 hours:

  • Contact the authorities and report the accident.
  • Gather information from witnesses, including their contact details.
  • Take photos and videos of the accident scene, vehicles, and injuries.
  • Seek medical attention immediately, even if you don't feel injured.
  • Notify Uber and your insurance company about the accident.
  • Document all communication with Uber and your insurance company.

Understanding Evidentiary Requirements

To pursue a personal injury claim or medical malpractice lawsuit, you'll need to gather evidence to support your case. This may include:

  • Police reports and accident reconstruction.
  • Medical records and expert testimony.
  • Witness statements and depositions.
  • Photographic and video evidence.
  • Expert opinions on liability and causation.

Liability in Uber Crash Malpractice Cases

In an Uber crash, multiple parties may be liable, including:

  • Uber (as the rideshare company).
  • The at-fault driver (if not an Uber employee).
  • The hospital or medical facility (for medical malpractice).
  • The manufacturer of the vehicle or its components.

Composite Case Study: Dual-Liability Settlement

John, a 35-year-old passenger, was involved in an Uber crash while riding to the hospital for a scheduled procedure. The driver, who was not an Uber employee, collided with another vehicle, causing John's car to crash into a nearby lamp post. John suffered severe injuries, including a broken femur and internal bleeding. He was rushed to the hospital, where he received subpar treatment, leading to further complications. John's medical bills exceeded $500,000, and he required multiple surgeries. In a dual-liability settlement, John's lawyers successfully argued that both Uber (for failing to properly screen and train their drivers) and the hospital (for medical malpractice) were responsible for John's injuries. The settlement award was $2.5 million, with $1.5 million allocated to medical expenses and $1 million for pain and suffering.
