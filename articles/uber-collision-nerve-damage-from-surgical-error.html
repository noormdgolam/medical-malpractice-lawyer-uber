Uber Collision Nerve Damage From Surgical Error: A Comprehensive Guide for Victims

Understanding the Dual-Liability of Rideshare and Medical Malpractice

When a rideshare accident results in nerve damage that is further exacerbated or caused by medical malpractice during treatment, victims face a complex web of liability. As a seasoned medical malpractice attorney, I will guide you through the process of navigating this dual-liability scenario, providing actionable step-by-step advice and explaining the evidentiary requirements necessary to secure a just settlement.

First 24 Hours: Immediate Action Required

  • Seek immediate medical attention for both the rideshare accident and any subsequent injuries or conditions that may have arisen during treatment.
  • Document everything, including witness statements, police reports, medical records, and any communication with the rideshare company or medical providers.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney who has experience in handling dual-liability cases.
  • Preserve evidence by saving all relevant documents, photos, and videos related to the accident and subsequent medical treatment.
  • Report the incident to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA) (https://www.nhtsa.gov/file-a-complaint) and the rideshare company's customer support.

Evidentiary Requirements for Medical Malpractice

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you must demonstrate the following:

  • Duty of care**: The medical provider had a duty to provide a standard level of care.
  • Breach of duty**: The medical provider failed to meet the standard of care, resulting in harm to the patient.
  • Causation**: The breach of duty caused the patient's injuries or conditions.
  • Damages**: The patient suffered actual harm or injuries as a result of the breach of duty.

Case Study: A Composite Rideshare and Medical Malpractice Settlement

Ms. Johnson, a 35-year-old mother of two, was involved in a rideshare accident while on her way home from work. The accident caused significant nerve damage, which was further exacerbated during her treatment at a local hospital. The hospital's surgical team failed to properly diagnose and treat her injuries, leading to permanent nerve damage and chronic pain.

A medical malpractice attorney, experienced in handling dual-liability cases, was retained to represent Ms. Johnson. Through a thorough investigation and expert testimony, it was established that the hospital's breach of duty caused Ms. Johnson's injuries, resulting in a settlement of $2.5 million.

Realistic Settlement Figures

According to a study by the Journal of the American Medical Association (JAMA), the average settlement for medical malpractice cases in the United States is around $400,000 (https://jamanetwork.com/journals/jama/fullarticle/2788441). However, cases involving dual-liability, such as rideshare and medical malpractice, can result in significantly higher settlements, as seen in the case study above.

Conclusion

Victims of rideshare accidents who suffer nerve damage and subsequent medical malpractice must take immediate action to preserve evidence and seek representation from an experienced medical malpractice attorney. By understanding the evidentiary requirements and the dual-liability of rideshare and medical malpractice, victims can secure a just settlement and hold responsible parties accountable for their actions.
