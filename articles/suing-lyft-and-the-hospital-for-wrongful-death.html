Suing Lyft And The Hospital For Wrongful Death: A Comprehensive Guide

Understanding Your Rights and Options

As a victim of a wrongful death involving a rideshare service like Lyft and a hospital, navigating the complexities of the law can be overwhelming. However, with the right guidance and support, you can hold those responsible accountable for their actions.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice for the First 24 Hours

  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney who has experience in handling cases involving rideshare services and hospitals. They will guide you through the process and ensure your rights are protected.
  • Document every detail of the incident, including the date, time, location, and any witnesses. Take photos and videos of any injuries or damage.
  • Report the incident to the police and file a police report. This will help establish a paper trail and provide evidence for your case.
  • Contact Lyft's customer service and report the incident. They may offer compensation or assistance, but be aware that this may not be a admission of liability.
  • Visit your local Department of Motor Vehicles (DMV) to report the incident and obtain a copy of the police report.
  • Seek medical attention immediately, even if you feel fine. Some injuries may not be apparent until later, and it's essential to document your injuries and treatment.

Evidentiary Requirements for a Successful Lawsuit

To succeed in a lawsuit against Lyft and the hospital, you'll need to gather and present evidence that demonstrates their liability. This may include:

  • Medical records and expert testimony to establish the hospital's negligence.
  • Testimony from witnesses, including the Lyft driver and any passengers.
  • Security footage from the hospital or rideshare service.
  • Expert testimony on the standard of care for hospitals and rideshare services.
  • Documentation of any injuries or damages, including medical bills and lost wages.

Composite Case Study: A Real-Life Example of a Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2020, a 35-year-old woman was involved in a car accident while riding in a Lyft vehicle. She was rushed to a nearby hospital, where she received inadequate treatment for her injuries. As a result, she suffered a permanent spinal cord injury and was left with significant medical bills and lost wages.

The woman's family hired a medical malpractice attorney who filed a lawsuit against both Lyft and the hospital. The attorney gathered evidence, including medical records, witness testimony, and security footage, to demonstrate the hospital's negligence and Lyft's liability for the accident.

The case was settled for $5 million, with $2.5 million allocated to the woman's medical bills and lost wages, and $2.5 million allocated to her pain and suffering.
