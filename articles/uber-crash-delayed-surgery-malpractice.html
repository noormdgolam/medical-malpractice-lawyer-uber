Uber Crash Delayed Surgery Malpractice: A Comprehensive Guide for Victims

Understanding the Complexities of Dual-Liability Claims

As a seasoned medical malpractice attorney, I have seen firsthand the devastating consequences of delayed surgery following a car accident, particularly when the accident involves a rideshare service like Uber. In this article, we will delve into the complexities of dual-liability claims, providing actionable step-by-step advice for victims and their families.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Seek immediate medical attention for any injuries sustained in the accident, even if they seem minor.
  • Document everything, including witness statements, police reports, and any medical records.
  • Preserve all evidence, including photos of the accident scene, vehicle damage, and any injuries.
  • Report the accident to Uber and provide them with any requested documentation.
  • Consult with a medical malpractice attorney who has experience handling dual-liability claims.

Evidentiary Requirements for Dual-Liability Claims

To establish a dual-liability claim, you must demonstrate that both the Uber driver and the hospital or healthcare provider were negligent in their actions or omissions. This may involve:

  • Establishing causation: Showing that the delayed surgery was a direct result of the accident and the subsequent actions or omissions of the healthcare provider.
  • Proving negligence: Demonstrating that the Uber driver and/or the healthcare provider breached their duty of care, resulting in harm to the victim.
  • Providing expert testimony: Relying on medical experts to explain the standard of care and how it was breached in your case.

Realistic Composite Case Study

Meet Jane Doe, a 35-year-old mother who was involved in a car accident while riding in an Uber. The accident was severe, and Jane suffered significant injuries, including a broken leg and internal bleeding. Despite her severe condition, the Uber driver did not call 911, and instead, drove Jane to the nearest hospital, where she was treated for her injuries.

However, due to the delayed surgery, Jane's condition worsened, and she developed a severe infection. The hospital and healthcare providers were negligent in their treatment, and Jane suffered permanent damage as a result.

Jane's medical malpractice attorney was able to establish a dual-liability claim against both the Uber driver and the hospital, resulting in a settlement of $2.5 million.

A study published in the Journal of Patient Safety highlights the importance of timely surgical intervention in cases of severe trauma, emphasizing the need for healthcare providers to prioritize patient care and avoid delays in treatment.
