Evidentiary Requirements for Expert Witnesses

Expert witnesses must meet specific evidentiary requirements to be admissible in court. These requirements include:

  • Qualifications: Expert witnesses must have specialized knowledge, skills, or experience that will assist the trier of fact in understanding the evidence or determining a fact in issue.
  • Expertise: Expert witnesses must be qualified in a particular field of expertise, such as medicine or accident reconstruction.
  • Reliability: Expert witnesses must be reliable and unbiased in their opinions and testimony.

Courts typically apply the Daubert standard (or Frye standard in some jurisdictions) to assess whether an expert�??s methodology is scientifically valid and applicable to the case at hand. This means the expert must be able to demonstrate that their techniques are peer�??reviewed, have a known error rate, and are generally accepted within the relevant professional community.
