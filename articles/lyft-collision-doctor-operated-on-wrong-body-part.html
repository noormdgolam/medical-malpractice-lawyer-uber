Lyft Collision Doctor Operated On Wrong Body Part: A Guide to Medical Malpractice and Dual-Liability Settlements

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

  • Seek immediate medical attention for your injuries, even if they seem minor.
  • Document everything: take photos of your injuries, the accident scene, and any property damage.
  • Report the incident to Lyft and the hospital where you received treatment.
  • Contact a medical malpractice attorney experienced in dual-liability cases.
  • Do not sign any medical releases or settlement agreements without consulting your attorney.

Evidentiary Requirements for Medical Malpractice Claims

To prove medical malpractice, you must demonstrate that the healthcare provider deviated from the standard of care and that this deviation caused your injuries. This typically requires:

  • A medical expert's opinion that the healthcare provider breached the standard of care.
  • Documentation of the healthcare provider's mistake, such as medical records or witness statements.
  • Proof of causation, linking the healthcare provider's mistake to your injuries.

Case Study: Dual-Liability Settlement

Meet Jane Doe, a 35-year-old mother who was involved in a Lyft accident while riding as a passenger. She was rushed to the hospital, where she underwent emergency surgery.

However, during the surgery, the doctor operated on the wrong body part, causing permanent nerve damage to Jane's left arm. The hospital and Lyft were both found liable for Jane's injuries.

After a lengthy investigation and settlement negotiations, Jane's medical malpractice attorney secured a $1.2 million settlement from the hospital and an additional $500,000 from Lyft. The total settlement amount was $1.7 million.

Real-Life Consequences of Medical Malpractice

Medical malpractice can have devastating consequences, including:

  • Permanent physical disability or disfigurement.
  • Emotional trauma and psychological distress.
  • Financial ruin due to medical expenses and lost wages.
