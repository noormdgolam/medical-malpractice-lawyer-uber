Lyft Collision Delay In Ordering Mri Or Ct Scan

By Editorial Team | Last Updated: July 17, 2026

Share:

Author: James Legal, Esq. | Last Updated: 07/17/2026

  • Delayed medical attention due to Lyft's negligence can have serious consequences for ride-sharing passengers involved in collisions, leading to reduced insurance payouts and damages in personal injury lawsuits.
  • California's comparative negligence law (Civil Code 1431.2) plays a crucial role in determining liability for damages, making it essential for riders to seek medical attention promptly and document their injuries.
  • Luckily, riders can take steps to secure fair compensation for their medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering, including filing insurance claims, seeking expert medical testimony, and navigating California's complex ride-sharing regulations (AB 2293).

Ride-Sharing Collisions: A Growing Concern

Ride-sharing companies like Lyft have revolutionized the way people travel, but with their rise comes a growing concern about ride-sharing collisions. According to a study by the National Transportation Safety Board (NTSB), ride-sharing collisions resulted in an estimated 1,259 fatalities in the United States between 2015 and 2018.

The data on ride-sharing collisions is staggering:

  • Between 2015 and 2018, ride-sharing collisions accounted for 3.2% of all traffic fatalities in the United States, compared to 2.2% in 2010-2012 (NTSB, 2019).
  • The annual number of ride-sharing collisions increased from 1,044 in 2015 to 1,249 in 2018, a 20% growth rate (National Traffic Safety Board, 2020).
  • California has the highest number of ride-sharing collisions, with 1 in 5 ride-sharing collisions occurring in the state (California Highway Patrol, 2020).

Lyft Collision Delay in Ordering MRI or CT Scans

Delayed medical attention in ride-sharing collisions can have severe consequences, particularly when critical tests like MRI or CT scans are not ordered promptly. Research suggests that delayed medical treatment can lead to increased morbidity and mortality rates, as well as higher healthcare costs (American College of Radiology, 2019).

Real-Life Examples: When Delayed Medical Attention Matters

The following case studies illustrate the importance of prompt medical attention and the potential consequences of delayed medical treatment:

  • In 2019, a Lyft passenger was involved in a collision in Los Angeles and suffered a spinal cord injury. Due to delayed medical attention, the passenger's claim was settled for $1 million less than it would have been if medical attention had been sought immediately (Los Angeles Times, 2020).
  • Another case in 2018 involved a Lyft driver who ran a red light and struck a pedestrian in San Francisco. The pedestrian suffered serious injuries, including a traumatic brain injury, but was not taken to the hospital until several hours after the incident. The pedestrian's damages were reduced by $500,000 due to delayed medical treatment (San Francisco Chronicle, 2019).

California's Comparative Negligence Law: A Key Factor in Ride-Sharing Liability

California's comparative negligence law (Civil Code 1431.2) allows jurors to apportion fault between parties involved in an accident. This law is particularly relevant in ride-sharing collisions, where Lyft's alleged negligence may have contributed to the incident.

According to a study by the American Association for Justice, ride-sharing companies like Lyft and Uber may be found liable for damages in up to 60% of ride-sharing collisions (American Association for Justice, 2020).

Navigating California's Ride-Sharing Regulations: AB 2293 and Insurance Coverage

California's ride-sharing regulations (AB 2293) require ride-sharing companies to provide insurance coverage for their drivers, including liability insurance and property damage coverage (California State Legislature, 2019). However, navigating this complex web of regulations can be challenging for riders seeking compensation for their medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

The table below highlights the key differences between Lyft's insurance coverage and traditional automobile insurance:

Conclusion

Delayed medical attention due to Lyft's negligence can have far-reaching consequences for ride-sharing passengers involved in collisions, including reduced insurance payouts and damages in personal injury lawsuits. California's comparative negligence law and the state's ride-sharing regulations play a crucial role in determining liability for damages.

Riders can take steps to secure fair compensation for their medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering by seeking medical attention promptly, documenting their injuries, and navigating California's complex web of regulations and insurance coverage.
