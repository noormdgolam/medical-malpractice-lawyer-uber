Uber Collision Emergency Room Triage Errors: A Guide for Victims

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

After an Uber collision, it's essential to prioritize your safety and well-being. Here are the steps to take within the first 24 hours:

  • Contact emergency services immediately if you're injured.
  • Call your insurance provider to report the incident.
  • Document the scene by taking photos and gathering witness statements.
  • Seek medical attention, even if you don't feel injured.
  • Report the incident to Uber and provide any necessary documentation.
  • Consult with an attorney specializing in medical malpractice and personal injury law.

Evidentiary Requirements

To build a strong case against the hospital and Uber, you'll need to gather and preserve evidence. This may include:

  • Medical records, including test results and treatment plans.
  • Police reports and witness statements.
  • Photos and videos of the accident scene.
  • Uber's accident report and any relevant documentation.
  • Expert testimony from medical professionals and accident reconstruction specialists.

Understanding the Liability of Uber and Hospitals

Both Uber and hospitals can be held liable for medical malpractice and negligence. In a dual-liability case, you may be able to recover damages from both parties.

Case Study: Smith v. Uber and St. Michael's Hospital

In 2019, a 35-year-old woman, Jane Smith, was involved in an Uber collision. She was rushed to St. Michael's Hospital, where she received inadequate emergency room triage. As a result, her injuries worsened, and she suffered long-term complications. Smith's family hired an attorney who successfully argued that both Uber and the hospital were liable for her damages. The case settled for $2.5 million, with $1.2 million coming from Uber and $1.3 million from the hospital.

According to the National Highway Traffic Safety Administration (NHTSA), motor vehicle crashes are a leading cause of injury and death in the United States. When these crashes involve ride-sharing services like Uber, the liability can be complex.

The American Medical Association (AMA) emphasizes the importance of clear and effective communication between healthcare providers and patients. In the event of an emergency, this communication is crucial in ensuring proper triage and treatment.
