Lyft Collision: Finding the Best Dual Liability Lawyer

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

After a Lyft collision, it's crucial to act quickly to protect your rights and ensure you receive the compensation you deserve. Here's a step-by-step guide for the first 24 hours:

  • Seek medical attention immediately, even if you feel fine.
  • Call the police and file a report.
  • Exchange information with the other parties involved, including the Lyft driver's information.
  • Take photos of the scene, including damage to vehicles and any injuries.
  • Document your injuries and any medical treatment you receive.
  • Contact your insurance company to report the incident.

Evidentiary Requirements

To build a strong case, you'll need to gather evidence, including:

  • Police report and incident report from Lyft.
  • Medical records and bills.
  • Photographic evidence of the scene and damage.
  • Witness statements.
  • Expert testimony from medical professionals and accident reconstructionists.

Understanding Dual Liability

Dual liability refers to the liability of both the Lyft driver and the hospital or medical facility for your injuries. In many cases, the Lyft driver is responsible for the initial collision, while the hospital or medical facility is responsible for any subsequent injuries or complications.

Case Study: $1.2 Million Settlement

Sarah, a 32-year-old mother of two, was a passenger in a Lyft vehicle that collided with another car. She suffered severe injuries, including a broken back and internal bleeding. The Lyft driver was at fault, but the hospital's negligence during her treatment exacerbated her injuries.

Our firm represented Sarah and her family, gathering evidence of the Lyft driver's negligence and the hospital's malpractice. We worked with medical experts to demonstrate the hospital's failure to provide proper care, including delayed treatment and inadequate pain management.

After a lengthy negotiation, we secured a $1.2 million settlement for Sarah and her family, covering her medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

Choosing the Right Lawyer

When selecting a dual liability lawyer, look for someone with experience in handling complex cases involving multiple parties. Consider the following factors:

  • Experience with Lyft collision cases.
  • Track record of successful settlements and verdicts.
  • Knowledge of medical malpractice law.
  • Strong communication and negotiation skills.

Conclusion

If you've been involved in a Lyft collision, it's essential to act quickly to protect your rights and ensure you receive the compensation you deserve. By following these steps, gathering evidence, and choosing the right lawyer, you can build a strong case and secure the justice you deserve.
