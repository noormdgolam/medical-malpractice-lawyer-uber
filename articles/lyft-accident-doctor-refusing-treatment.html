Lyft Accident Doctor Refusing Treatment: A Comprehensive Guide for Victims

Understanding Your Rights and Options

As a passenger in a Lyft accident, you may be entitled to compensation for medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering. However, if the doctor refuses treatment or fails to properly diagnose your injuries, your case may become more complex.

Actionable Step-by-Step Advice (First 24 Hours)

  • Contact the police and report the accident as soon as possible.
  • Seek medical attention immediately, even if you don't feel injured.
  • Document everything: take photos of the accident scene, your injuries, and any property damage.
  • Get the contact information of any witnesses.
  • Contact your Lyft driver and the company to report the accident.
  • Do not give a statement to the insurance company without consulting an attorney.
  • Keep a record of all medical treatment and expenses.

Evidentiary Requirements

To prove a medical malpractice case, you'll need to establish the following elements:

  • Duty: The doctor owed you a duty of care.
  • Breach: The doctor breached that duty by failing to provide proper treatment or diagnosis.
  • Causation: The doctor's breach caused your injuries.
  • Damages: You suffered actual damages as a result of the doctor's breach.

Expert Testimony and Evidence

Expert testimony from medical professionals can be crucial in establishing the doctor's breach of duty and causation. You'll also need to gather evidence, such as:

  • Medical records and bills.
  • Testimony from witnesses, including the Lyft driver and passengers.
  • Photos and videos of the accident scene and your injuries.
  • Expert opinions from medical professionals and accident reconstruction specialists.

Composite Case Study: Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

A 35-year-old woman was involved in a Lyft accident and suffered a severe head injury. She was taken to a local hospital, where the doctor refused to provide proper treatment and diagnosis. The woman suffered long-term cognitive and emotional damage as a result. She hired a medical malpractice attorney and filed a lawsuit against both Lyft and the hospital. After a lengthy settlement negotiation, the woman received a total of $2.5 million in compensation, with $1.2 million coming from Lyft and $1.3 million from the hospital.

Realistic Settlement Expectations

Settlement amounts can vary widely depending on the severity of the injuries, the level of negligence, and the jurisdiction. In general, you can expect to receive compensation for:

  • Medical expenses, including past and future treatment.
  • Lost wages and earning capacity.
  • Pain and suffering, including emotional and psychological distress.
