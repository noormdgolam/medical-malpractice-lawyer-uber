Uber Passenger Permanent Disability From Medical Malpractice: A Comprehensive Guide

Introduction

As an Uber passenger, you trust that the driver and medical professionals will prioritize your safety and well-being. However, medical malpractice can occur even in the most unexpected situations, leaving you with permanent disabilities. If you've suffered an injury while riding with Uber, it's essential to understand your rights and the steps to take to seek compensation.

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

  • Seek Medical Attention Immediately: Even if you feel fine after the incident, it's crucial to get checked by a medical professional. Some injuries may not be immediately apparent, and a timely medical evaluation can help prevent further complications.
  • Document Everything: Keep a record of all medical treatments, hospital stays, and any correspondence with medical professionals. This documentation will be vital in building your case.
  • Report the Incident to Uber: Inform Uber about the incident, providing as much detail as possible. This will help initiate an investigation and potentially lead to compensation.
  • Consult with a Medical Malpractice Attorney: Reach out to a qualified medical malpractice attorney who can guide you through the process and help you navigate the complexities of your case.

Evidentiary Requirements

To establish a medical malpractice claim, you'll need to provide evidence that the medical professional(s) deviated from the standard of care, resulting in your injury. This may include:

  • Medical Records: Obtain and preserve all medical records, including hospital stays, surgeries, and any subsequent treatments.
  • Expert Testimony: Engage with medical experts who can testify that the medical professional(s) failed to meet the standard of care.
  • Witness Statements: Collect statements from witnesses, including the Uber driver, other passengers, or medical staff.
  • Photographic Evidence: Gather any relevant photographs or videos of the incident, injury, or medical treatment.

Case Study: Rideshare/Hospital Dual-Liability Settlement

In 2019, a 35-year-old woman suffered a spinal cord injury while riding with Uber. The driver, who was speeding, lost control of the vehicle, and the woman was rushed to the hospital. During her treatment, medical staff failed to properly diagnose and treat her injury, leading to permanent paralysis. The woman filed a lawsuit against both Uber and the hospital, alleging dual liability for her injuries. After a lengthy trial, the court awarded her a $5 million settlement, with $2.5 million attributed to Uber's negligence and $2.5 million to the hospital's medical malpractice.
