Lyft Passenger Coma Due To Hospital Error: A Guide to Seeking Justice

What to Do in the First 24 Hours

When a hospital error results in a life-altering injury, such as a coma, it's essential to act quickly to protect your rights and seek compensation.

  • Contact a Medical Malpractice Attorney: Reach out to a seasoned medical malpractice attorney as soon as possible. They will guide you through the process and help you navigate the complex legal landscape.
  • Gather Medical Records: Request a copy of your medical records from the hospital, including any relevant test results, diagnoses, and treatment plans.
  • Document the Incident: Write down everything you remember about the incident, including the events leading up to the hospital visit and the treatment you received.
  • Preserve Evidence: Take photos of any injuries, bruises, or other physical evidence related to the incident.
  • Notify the Authorities: If the hospital error resulted in a serious injury, notify the relevant authorities, such as the state medical board or the hospital's internal affairs department.

Evidentiary Requirements

To pursue a medical malpractice claim, you'll need to establish that the hospital's negligence caused your injury. This typically requires:

  • Duty of Care: Prove that the hospital owed you a duty of care, which includes providing a safe and competent level of medical treatment.
  • Breach of Duty: Show that the hospital breached this duty of care through negligence, such as a failure to diagnose or treat a condition properly.
  • Causation: Demonstrate that the hospital's breach of duty caused your injury, such as a coma.
  • Damages: Establish the extent of your damages, including medical expenses, lost wages, and pain and suffering.

Case Study: A $1.5 Million Settlement

A 35-year-old woman was involved in a car accident while riding in a Lyft. She was taken to a local hospital, where she received treatment for her injuries. However, the hospital staff failed to properly diagnose and treat her condition, resulting in a coma that lasted several weeks.

The woman's family hired a medical malpractice attorney, who investigated the incident and gathered evidence to support their claim. The attorney demonstrated that the hospital's negligence caused the woman's injury and established the extent of her damages.

The case ultimately settled for $1.5 million, which will help the woman and her family cover her ongoing medical expenses and lost wages.

"This settlement is a testament to the importance of holding hospitals accountable for their actions," said the woman's attorney. "We will continue to fight for justice on behalf of our clients and ensure that they receive the compensation they deserve."
